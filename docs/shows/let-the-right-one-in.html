What It Feels Like

Eleanor is 12. She has been 12 for a long time. Her father Mark does what he must to feed her. Showtime's adaptation of the Swedish vampire story: parent-child love, the burden of care, vampire as family drama.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Demián Bichir as Mark: the father's desperation. Madison Taylor Baez as Eleanor: eternal child. The performances ground the horror.

World Building (8/10)

New York City: the apartments, the vampire underground, the eternal child's burden. Horror world with human stakes.

Cinematography (7/10)

Showtime production: New York nights, the vampire condition visualized. Atmospheric horror.

Visual Spectacle (6/10)

Vampire horror elements: the feeding, the eternal child. Moderate spectacle.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Parental love, the cost of care, eternal childhood. The show explores family through vampire metaphor.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

What is Eleanor? Can she be saved? The drive is horror family drama.

Narrative Path & Resolution (5/10)

Cancelled after one season. The story is incomplete. A tragedy of potential.
