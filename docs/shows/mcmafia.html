What It Feels Like

Alex Godman is a London banker trying to stay legitimate. His family is Russian exile royalty with organized crime connections. When his uncle is murdered, the legitimate world and the criminal world collide. James Norton leads the global crime thriller.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

James Norton as Alex: the man between worlds. The international cast creates the global crime network. Conviction performances.

World Building (9/10)

Global organized crime: London, Moscow, Tel Aviv, Mumbai. The world is interconnected criminal infrastructure.

Cinematography (8/10)

BBC production: international locations, the visual language of global crime. Each city distinct.

Visual Spectacle (6/10)

The spectacle is international scale: money, violence, power across borders. Moderate action.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Legitimacy vs. crime, family obligation, the global economy as criminal infrastructure. The show explores modern capitalism.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Alex's descent into the family business. Each episode pulls him deeper. Crime thriller structure.

Narrative Path & Resolution (7/10)

Complete miniseries. The story resolves with moral complexity. Season 2 planned but unproduced.
