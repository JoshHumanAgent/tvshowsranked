Vikings

2013 - 2020 • History • 89 Episodes

Score: 7.18
Historical Drama, Action

What It Feels Like

Ragnar Lothbrok is a farmer who becomes a legendary Viking raider. He sails west to England, builds an empire, and creates a dynasty that will reshape history. Travis Fimmel is Ragnar—charismatic, ambitious, ultimately tragic. Michael Hirst created History Channel's first scripted drama: Viking legend as family saga, with blood, betrayal, and gods.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Travis Fimmel's Ragnar is the center for the first four seasons: the farmer who becomes king, the king who loses everything. Katheryn Winnick as Lagertha provides the warrior queen energy. Clive Standen, Gustaf Skarsgård, Alexander Ludwig—the sons and rivals create a dynastic saga. The performances embrace the epic.

World Building (9/10)

Viking Age Scandinavia: Kattegat, the ships, the raids, the gods. England as target and destination. The world expands across seasons—the Mediterranean, Iceland, the Rus. The historical detail is researched while embracing legend.

Cinematography (8/10)

History Channel drama: the ships are majestic, the battles are staged with scale, the landscapes are beautiful. The style is epic television—Game of Thrones without dragons. The battles are visceral and clear.

Visual Spectacle (8/10)

Naval battles, raids, duels, sieges. The spectacle is historical warfare: shield walls, longboats, brutal combat. The budget is visible on screen. This is action television with historical weight.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Ambition, legacy, the gods' will, whether power corrupts or reveals, family vs. empire. The show explores these through Ragnar and his sons. The themes are epic but accessible.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Ragnar's rise, then his sons' expansion. Each season has raids, betrayals, wars. The drive is dynastic—who will inherit, who will survive. The pacing is propulsive; the body count is high.

Narrative Path & Resolution (7/10)

Six seasons with conclusion. Ragnar's story ends in season 4; his sons continue. The finale provides closure for the saga. The show outlived its protagonist but maintained quality.
