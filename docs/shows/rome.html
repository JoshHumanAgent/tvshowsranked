What It Feels Like to Watch

HBO's Rome is the Republic's fall through two soldiers' eyes—Lucius Vorenus and Titus Pullo, legionnaires who stumble through Caesar's assassination, Antony and Cleopatra, and the rise of Augustus. History happens around them; they survive it.

Kevin McKidd's Vorenus is duty, honor, and rage. Ray Stevenson's Pullo is chaos, appetite, and loyalty. Their friendship is the show's heart—two men from different worlds bound by violence and survival. Around them: Caesar (Ciarán Hinds), Antony (James Purefoy), Augustus (Simon Woods), Atia (Polly Walker).

Two seasons before cancellation. The Republic falls, the Empire rises, the friends survive—barely. The finale provides closure; the cancellation cuts short what could have been.
