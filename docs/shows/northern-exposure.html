Northern Exposure

1990 - 1995 • CBS • 110 Episodes

Score: 7.25
Comedy-Drama

What It Feels Like

Joel Fleischman is a New York doctor who agrees to work in Alaska to pay for medical school. He ends up in Cicely, a town of eccentrics: a pilot, a DJ, a former astronaut, a Native American shaman. The show is Magical realism in small-town Alaska: philosophical, whimsical, the 90s answer to Twin Peaks without the darkness. One of television's great ensemble dramedies.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Rob Morrow as Joel is the straight man surrounded by eccentrics. Janine Turner as Maggie, John Corbett as Chris, Barry Corbin as Maurice, Cynthia Geary as Shelly—the town is a gallery of distinct personalities. The performances embrace the show's magical realist tone.

World Building (10/10)

Cicely, Alaska: the town, the radio station, the bar, the wilderness. The world is complete and specific—the seasons, the isolation, the indigenous presence, the eccentric history. The town is a character; the setting is the show.

Cinematography (6/10)

Early 90s TV aesthetics: functional, the beauty of Alaska captured when possible. The style serves the characters and the philosophical musings. Not distinctive, but appropriate.

Visual Spectacle (5/10)

The spectacle is Alaska: the wilderness, the seasons, the Aurora Borealis. The show uses the landscape as backdrop for philosophical moments. Nature as spectacle, character as focus.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Community, the clash between urban and rural values, indigenous culture and white settlement, the search for meaning in isolation. The show explores philosophy through whimsy—each episode a fable about how to live. Intellectual without being pretentious.

Narrative Drive (6/10)

Episodic with ongoing development: Joel's contract, various romances, the town's events. The drive is gentle—this is not plot television, it's character television. The pleasure is returning to Cicely, not seeing what happens next.

Narrative Path & Resolution (7/10)

Six seasons. Joel eventually leaves Alaska; the show continued without him but lost its center. The complete run is available; the ending is appropriate if not definitive. The show's peak was seasons 2-4.
