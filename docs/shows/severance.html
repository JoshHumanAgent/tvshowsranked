Severance

2022 - Present • Apple TV+ • Ongoing

Score: 8.10
Sci-Fi, Thriller

What It Feels Like

Lumon Industries has a procedure: severance. Your work memories are separated from your outside memories. You are two people. Mark leads a team of severed workers. Then he finds a clue from the outside. Ben Stiller directs the sci-fi thriller about work-life balance made literal horror.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Adam Scott as Mark: the innie and the outie. Patricia Arquette as Harmony: the manager. John Turturro, Christopher Walken, Britt Lower. The performances capture the dissociation—two selves in one body.

World Building (10/10)

Lumon: the office, the severed floor, the outside, the departments that don't know each other. The world is corporate horror—work separated from life, literally. Every detail matters.

Cinematography (9/10)

Apple TV+ production: the office as liminal space, the corridors, the weird geometry. The style is Kubrickian—symmetry, unease, beauty made unsettling.

Visual Spectacle (6/10)

The spectacle is the office: the corridors, the break room, the mysterious departments. The "spectacle" is the concept—watching two selves in one body.

Conceptual Density (10/10)

Work-life balance made literal, whether we can escape ourselves, what corporations own, the self as property, identity as division. The show explores these through sci-fi horror.

Narrative Drive (10/10)

What is Lumon hiding? What happens on the severed floor? Who are the outies? The structure is mystery—every episode reveals more. The drive is relentless.

Narrative Path & Resolution (5/10)

Ongoing. Season 1 ends on cliffhanger; season 2 in production. The mystery is unresolved. The journey is spectacular; the destination is pending.
