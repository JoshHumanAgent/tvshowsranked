What It Feels Like to Watch

Selina Meyer (Julia Louis-Dreyfus) is Vice President of the United States. She thought it would be powerful. It isn't. She's ignored, sidelined, and undermined by everyone from her own staff to foreign dignitaries. And she's determined to become President.

Armando Iannucci's In the Loop was the template: rapid-fire dialogue, overlapping conversations, insults that land like bullets. Veep refines the formula. Louis-Dreyfus is extraordinary—a character who is simultaneously petty, ambitious, vulnerable, and cruel. The supporting cast is uniformly brilliant, each with their own agenda.

The show's genius is in its cynicism. Politics is presented as a game where everyone loses, but the losing is hilarious. The insults are legendary. The pacing is frenetic. By the final season, the show has become a tragedy disguised as a comedy.
