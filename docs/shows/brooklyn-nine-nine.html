Brooklyn Nine-Nine

2013 - 2021 • NBC/Fox • 153 Episodes

Score: 7.35
Comedy, Police

What It Feels Like

Jake Peralta is a detective who doesn't take anything seriously. Captain Holt is his new boss who takes everything seriously. Michael Schur and Dan Goor created police comedy that's actually funny: cases solved with jokes, found family in the precinct.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Andy Samberg as Jake: juvenile detective with skill. Andre Braugher as Captain Holt: deadpan perfection. The ensemble (Terry Crews, Melissa Fumero, Stephanie Beatriz) creates the precinct as family.

World Building (7/10)

The 99th precinct: the bullpen, the cases, the relationships. The world is workplace sitcom—specific through character.

Cinematography (5/10)

Single-camera sitcom: bright, fast-paced, the visual language of network comedy. Functional.

Visual Spectacle (2/10)

The spectacle is occasionally the cases—murder mysteries played for comedy. Light spectacle.

Conceptual Density (4/10)

Friendship, workplace found family, growing up. Light themes. The show addressed police issues in later seasons but mostly it's comedy.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Episodic cases, serialized relationships. Jake's growth; Holt's humanity revealed. The drive is character-driven comedy.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Eight seasons, complete. The flash-forward finale gives everyone their ending. A satisfying sitcom conclusion.
