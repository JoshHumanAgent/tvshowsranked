Crazyhead

2016 • E4 • 6 Episodes

Score: 7.15
Comedy, Horror

What It Feels Like

Amy thinks she's having psychotic episodes. Actually, she's seeing demons. Raquel shows up and explains: they're demon hunters. The two young women become partners in exorcising demons from their British town. Cara Theobold and Susan Wokoma star in Howard Overman's horror-comedy: Buffy meets Misfits with British sensibility.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Cara Theobold as Amy: the reluctant hunter learning she's not crazy. Susan Wokoma as Raquel: the experienced demon hunter with her own issues. Their chemistry drives the show. The performances balance horror and comedy.

World Building (7/10)

British suburban demon hunting: the possessed, the rituals, the hierarchy waiting to emerge. The world is small-scale but functional—demons in the margins of ordinary life.

Cinematography (5/10)

E4 production: British comedy-horror aesthetics, the demons rendered with TV budget, the suburbs as setting. The style is functional—the comedy and horror carry it.

Visual Spectacle (4/10)

Demon possession, exorcisms, the creature effects. The spectacle is TV-budget horror—the ideas carry what budget can't show. Comedy-horror doesn't need spectacle.

Conceptual Density (6/10)

Mental health as metaphor, female friendship, the supernatural as explanation for the unexplainable. The show treats demon hunting as therapy. The themes are present amid the jokes.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Demon of the week with serialized mystery: what's the hierarchy, why Amy, can they stop the apocalypse? The structure is Buffy-esque—episodic cases building to bigger threat.

Narrative Path & Resolution (5/10)

One season (6 episodes). The story continues in implied future seasons that never came. The potential was there; the continuation wasn't. Cult favorite, incomplete.
