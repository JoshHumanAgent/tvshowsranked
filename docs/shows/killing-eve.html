Killing Eve

2018 - 2022 • BBC America • 32 Episodes

Score: 7.45
Thriller, Dark Comedy

What It Feels Like

Eve Polastri is an MI5 analyst obsessed with female assassins. Villanelle is a Russian assassin who kills with style. They become obsessed with each other—cat and mouse, attraction and violence. Sandra Oh and Jodie Comer star in Phoebe Waller-Bridge's adaptation: spy thriller as black comedy.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (10/10)

Sandra Oh as Eve: the analyst becoming what she hunts. Jodie Comer as Villanelle: the assassin with style and humor. The chemistry between them defines the show. Comer's Villanelle is iconic.

World Building (7/10)

European espionage: MI5, the Twelve, the locations. The world serves the cat-and-mouse—the specifics matter less than the chase.

Cinematography (8/10)

BBC America production: European locations, fashion-forward assassin, the kills staged as dark comedy. The style is bright where spy thrillers are usually dark.

Visual Spectacle (7/10)

The spectacle is Villanelle's kills: stylish, funny, brutal. Each assassination is a fashion shoot with death. The spectacle serves the character.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Obsession, the attraction to violence, whether hunter and hunted can love each other, identity as performance. The show explores these through black comedy.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

Eve hunts Villanelle. Villanelle hunts Eve. They meet. The drive is cat-and-mouse with romantic tension. Season 1 is perfect; later seasons vary.

Narrative Path & Resolution (5/10)

Four seasons. Season 1 is among the best television of its era. Seasons 2-4 diminish in quality. The finale disappointed many. The journey exceeds the destination.
