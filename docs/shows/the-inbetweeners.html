Comedy, Teen

What It Feels Like

Will, Simon, Jay, and Neil are suburban sixth-formers. They're not cool kids, not nerds—they're inbetween. Damon Beesley and Iain Morris created the most accurate teen comedy: the awkwardness, the lying, the desperate pursuit of sex, the complete failure.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Simon Bird as Will: posh and awkward. Blake Harrison as Neil: the idiot. Joe Thomas as Simon: desperate love. James Buckley as Jay: the liar. Perfect ensemble misery.

World Building (8/10)

Suburban sixth-form: the school, the bus stop, the caravan club. British teen life rendered in cringe.

Cinematography (6/10)

E4 production: handheld, naturalistic, the visual language of embarrassment.

Visual Spectacle (1/10)

No spectacle. This is suburbia. The spectacle is watching them fail.

Conceptual Density (6/10)

Teen masculinity, friendship, sexual desperation. The show explores adolescence through comedy of pain.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Will they get girls/get drunk/get respect? No. The drive is laughing at their failures.

Narrative Path >Resolution (9/10)

Three seasons + two films, complete. School ends; friendship continues. Perfect teen comedy.
