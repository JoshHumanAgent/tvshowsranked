Heartstopper

2022 - Present • Netflix • Ongoing

Score: 7.55
Romance, LGBTQ+

What It Feels Like

Nick and Charlie are British schoolboys who fall in love. That's it. That's the plot. Alice Oseman adapts her graphic novel: queer joy, first love, friendship. The show that made people believe in romance again.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Kit Connor as Nick: the popular boy who figures out he's bi. Joe Locke as Charlie: the out boy with anxiety. The ensemble creates the friend group. The performances capture teen authenticity.

World Building (8/10)

British secondary school: the forms, the rugby, the friendships, the coming out. The world is school as both prison and possibility. Truham Grammar for love.

Cinematography (8/10)

Netflix production: the animation style from the graphic novel, the softness, the leaves floating when Nick falls for Charlie. The style is gentle—this is romance without harshness.

Visual Spectacle (2/10)

No spectacle. This is teen romance: school, friends, first love. The "spectacle" is emotional—the joy of being seen, the safety of love.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Coming out, first love, friendship, mental health, queer joy. The show explores these through gentle romance. There's no trauma—only love.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Will they get together? They do. Will they stay together? The structure is romance—the drive is relationship. Each season develops the relationships.

Narrative Path & Resolution (6/10)

Ongoing. Three seasons complete; more planned. The story continues. The romance is the point—there's no endpoint except love.
