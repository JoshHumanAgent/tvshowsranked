Smallville

2001 - 2011 • The CW • 217 Episodes

Score: 6.90
Superhero, Drama

What It Feels Like

Clark Kent in high school. He's not Superman yet. He's learning about his powers, fighting meteor freaks, hiding his identity from Lana and Chloe. The show that started the superhero TV boom: "no tights, no flights."

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Tom Welling as Clark: earnest farm boy becoming hero. Michael Rosenbaum as Lex: the friendship that becomes rivalry. Allison Mack as Chloe; Kristen Kreuk as Lana. The cast grows over ten seasons.

World Building (8/10)

Smallville, Kansas: the farm, the town, the Luthor mansion, the Daily Planet. The world expands from high school to Metropolis, from episodic to serialized.

Cinematography (5/10)

WB/CW production: Vancouver for Kansas, the visual language of 2000s superhero TV. Functional but dated.

Visual Spectacle (5/10)

Super speed, heat vision, Kryptonite effects. The spectacle improved over ten seasons but remained TV-scale.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Identity, responsibility, the cost of secrets, Lex's descent. The show explores Superman mythology through adolescence.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Villain of the week + mythology building. The drive is Clark becoming Superman.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Ten seasons, complete. Clark becomes Superman in the finale. Earned conclusion to decade-long journey.
