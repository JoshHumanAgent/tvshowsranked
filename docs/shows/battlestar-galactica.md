## What It Feels Like to Watch

Battlestar Galactica redefined what science fiction television could be, bringing philosophical depth and human drama to space opera. Following the last surviving humans fleeing robotic Cylons, the show balances spectacular space battles with intimate character studies.

You watch for the unexpected turns—characters you trust who turn out to be Cylons, moral compromises that blur the line between heroes and villains. The show asks difficult questions about what makes us human when machines can believe they're alive.

Across 75 episodes, the series maintains surprising consistency, though late-season mythology gets convoluted. The characters remain compelling even when the plot mechanics falter, anchored by Mary McDonnell and Edward James Olmos as leaders navigating impossible choices.
