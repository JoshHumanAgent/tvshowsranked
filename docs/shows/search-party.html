What It Feels Like to Watch

Four Brooklyn millennials—a writer, a PR drone, an activist, and a film student—have a friend who goes missing. Instead of calling the police, they decide to investigate themselves. What begins as a millennial comedy about entitled twentysomethings slowly descends into something darker: a noir thriller about people who discover they're capable of terrible things.

The show evolves in ways you don't expect. Alia Shawkat's Dory is the emotional center—a woman who realizes she's been sleepwalking through her life and, in solving this mystery, might actually become someone. The supporting cast—John Early, Meredith Garretson, Cole Escola—build characters who start as types and become something more.

The tonal range is remarkable. Season 1 is almost a sitcom. Season 2 is a conspiracy thriller. Season 3 is... what the hell is season 3? The show refuses to stay in one lane, and that's its genius.
