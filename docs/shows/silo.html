What It Feels Like to Watch

Hugh Howey's Wool adapted for television—140 stories underground, humanity preserved in a silo, secrets beneath secrets. Rebecca Ferguson's Juliette is an engineer who becomes sheriff, asking questions that get people killed. The show builds mystery methodically: what's outside? Why are we here? Who's in charge?

The silo is rendered with claustrophobic specificity—the spiral staircase connecting levels, the mechanical department, IT's hidden floors, the screen showing the toxic outside world. The first season focuses on Juliette's investigation, the second expands to the silo's history and its secrets.

Rebecca Ferguson carries the show with physical commitment and emotional depth. The supporting cast (David Oyelowo, Common, Tim Robbins) provides texture. The mystery structure ensures every answer opens new questions.

Two seasons and continuing. The show demonstrates that contained-world science fiction can be as compelling as interstellar epics—it's about the society we build when we're all that's left.
