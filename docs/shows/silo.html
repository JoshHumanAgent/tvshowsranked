Silo

2023 - Present • Apple TV+ • Ongoing

Score: 7.70
Sci-Fi, Mystery

What It Feels Like

Ten thousand people live in a silo underground. The outside is toxic. They don't know why they're there or who built it. Juliette is an engineer who becomes sheriff and investigates. Hugh Howey's Wool adapted: dystopian mystery as escape thriller.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Rebecca Ferguson as Juliette: the engineer who keeps asking questions. The ensemble creates the silo's society. The performances capture the paranoia.

World Building (10/10)

The silo: the levels, the departments, the rules, the history. The world is a complete underground society—every level has purpose, every rule has reason. Mystery upon mystery.

Cinematography (9/10)

Apple TV+ production: the silo rendered with claustrophobic detail, the outside through the viewport, the stairs connecting everything. The style is oppressive—the silo is character.

Visual Spectacle (7/10)

The spectacle is the silo: the staircase, the levels, the outside viewed through the lens. The production builds a world underground.

Conceptual Density (9/10)

Who controls the silo? Why are they there? What happened outside? The show explores secrecy, authority, whether knowledge is dangerous. The mystery is everything.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

Why are they in the silo? Each episode reveals more. The structure is mystery—revelation upon revelation. The drive is the truth.

Narrative Path & Resolution (5/10)

Ongoing. Season 1 reveals some truth; more seasons planned. The mystery continues. Adaptation in progress.
