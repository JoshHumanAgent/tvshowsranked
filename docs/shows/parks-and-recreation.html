Parks and Recreation

2009 - 2015 • NBC • 125 Episodes

Score: 7.60
Comedy, Mockumentary

What It Feels Like

Leslie Knope works in the Pawnee Parks Department. She loves her town too much. Her coworkers are various levels of cynical. Greg Daniels and Michael Schur created the anti-cynicism comedy: government work made noble, friendship made essential.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Amy Poehler as Leslie: relentless optimism. Adam Scott as Ben: the straight man won over. Nick Offerman as Ron Swanson: libertarian made lovable. Aziz Ansari, Rashida Jones, Chris Pratt, Aubrey Plaza. Perfect ensemble.

World Building (8/10)

Pawnee, Indiana: the town, the government, the citizens, the history. The world is built through mockumentary detail—every town has its lore.

Cinematography (5/10)

Mockumentary style inherited from The Office: talking heads, handheld. Functional comedy direction.

Visual Spectacle (1/10)

No spectacle. This is local government work. The "spectacle" is enthusiasm.

Conceptual Density (6/10)

Public service, friendship, optimism vs. cynicism, local government. The themes are explored through relentless positivity.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Leslie's projects and career; relationships developing. The structure evolves from episodic to serialized—the show found its voice in season 2.

Narrative Path & Resolution (9/10)

Seven seasons, complete. The flash-forward finale is perfect. Every character gets their ending. A model sitcom conclusion.
