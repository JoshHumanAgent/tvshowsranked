The Good Place

2016 - 2020 • NBC • 53 Episodes

Score: 7.95
Comedy, Fantasy

What It Feels Like

Eleanor Shellstrop has died. She's in the Good Place. Except she wasn't good—she was a terrible person who doesn't belong. Michael Schur created sitcom as philosophy: ethics made comedy, the afterlife as character study.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Kristen Bell as Eleanor: terrible person learning better. Ted Danson as Michael: the architect revealed. William Jackson Harper as Chidi: ethics professor made real. The performances ground the absurdity.

World Building (10/10)

The Good Place (and Bad Place): the neighborhood, the rules, the point system, the afterlife bureaucracy. The world is philosophy made visual—every detail has purpose.

Cinematography (7/10)

Bright colors, suburban utopia, the visual language of heaven made sitcom. The style serves the premise.

Visual Spectacle (5/10)

The spectacle is the afterlife: demons, voids, the judge. But mostly this is people talking in a neighborhood.

Conceptual Density (10/10)

Ethics, redemption, whether people can change, what we owe to each other, the meaning of eternal life. The show is philosophy made accessible.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

Each season reinvents itself; the twists are planned. The drive is understanding—what is this place, why am I here, can I be better?

Narrative Path & Resolution (10/10)

Four seasons, complete. The ending is perfect—philosophers get their answer. One of the best series finales ever made.
