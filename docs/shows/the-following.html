The Following

2013 - 2015 • Fox • 45 Episodes

Score: 7.15
Thriller, Crime

What It Feels Like

Joe Carroll was a literature professor who became a serial killer, inspired by Edgar Allan Poe. He built a cult of followers who will kill for him. Ryan Hardy is the FBI agent who caught him—and who Carroll now torments from prison. Kevin Bacon is Hardy; James Purefoy is Carroll. Cat and mouse, cult dynamics, elaborate kills. Kevin Williamson's Fox thriller: stylish, violent, formula-testing.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (7/10)

Kevin Bacon is the damaged FBI agent, carrying the show through its convolutions. James Purefoy as Joe Carroll is charismatic and terrifying—a villain who seduces through intellect. The followers vary in quality. The leads carry the premise.

World Building (7/10)

Carroll's cult: followers everywhere, embedded in society, activated by code words and Poe references. The world is paranoid—anyone could be a follower. The mythology expands across seasons, sometimes beyond credibility.

Cinematography (6/10)

Network thriller aesthetics: murders staged for impact, cult gatherings in dramatic locations. The style serves the premise without distinguishing. The violence is network-allowed but pushes boundaries.

Visual Spectacle (5/10)

The spectacle is violence: elaborate kills, mass murders, cult rituals. The murders are staged for maximum impact. The spectacle serves the thriller—cultist attacks, Carroll's theatricality.

Conceptual Density (5/10)

Cults, obsession, literature as inspiration for violence, the relationship between hunter and hunted. The themes are present but secondary to the thriller mechanics. Poe is decorative more than meaningful.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Relentless: Carroll escapes, cultists attack, Hardy pursues. The show is designed for momentum—each episode ends with a twist or escalation. The structure is cat-and-mouse with ever-increasing stakes.

Narrative Path & Resolution (5/10)

Three seasons, cancelled. The escalating cult premise became unsustainable—how many followers could one man have? The show began to strain credibility. The ending was produced but the concept had exhausted itself.
