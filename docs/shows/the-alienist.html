The Alienist

2018 - 2020 • TNT • 18 Episodes

Score: 7.18
Period, Crime

What It Feels Like

1896 New York. A serial killer targets boy prostitutes. Dr. Laszlo Kreizler—a criminal psychologist, an "alienist"—investigates. With him: a newspaper illustrator and a police secretary. Caleb Carr's novel adapted: the birth of criminal profiling, Gilded Age New York, darkness beneath the wealth.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Daniel Brühl as Kreizler: the alienist, damaged and brilliant. Luke Evans as Moore: the illustrator drawn into darkness. Dakota Fanning as Sara Howard: the secretary who wants to be detective. The performances capture period psychology.

World Building (9/10)

1896 New York: the mansions, the tenements, the corruption, the poverty. The world is Gilded Age in all its contradiction—wealth and squalor. The period detail is immersive.

Cinematography (8/10)

TNT production: New York rendered in gaslight and shadow, the period photographed with atmosphere. The style is Victorian noir—the past as foreign country.

Visual Spectacle (6/10)

The spectacle is period: the mansions, the slums, the crime scenes. The production value serves the investigation—not action but atmosphere. Victorian true crime.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Criminal psychology's birth, what makes killers, childhood trauma, wealth's indifference to poverty's suffering. The show explores these through the first profiling case.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

The killer must be found: clues, profiles, investigations, danger. The structure is serial killer thriller—each episode reveals more about the perpetrator. The drive is constant.

Narrative Path & Resolution (7/10)

Two seasons, adapting two Carr novels. Each season resolves its case; the characters continue. The show is complete within its seasons. Period crime done right.
