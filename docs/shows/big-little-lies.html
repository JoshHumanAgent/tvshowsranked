Big Little Lies

2017 - 2019 • HBO • 14 Episodes

Score: 7.75
Drama, Mystery

What It Feels Like

Monterey, California. Wealthy mothers, perfect lives, hidden bruises. Someone dies at school trivia night. Liane Moriarty's novel adapted by David E. Kelley: the mothers' secrets, the husbands' violence, the children who witness everything. Reese Witherspoon, Nicole Kidman, Shailene Woodley lead.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (10/10)

Reese Witherspoon as Madeline: the organizer, the meddler. Nicole Kidman as Celeste: the abused wife who doesn't see it. Shailene Woodley as Jane: the single mother with a secret. Laura Dern as Renata. The ensemble is HBO prestige at its finest.

World Building (9/10)

Monterey: the beautiful houses, the ocean, the perfect schools, the violence behind closed doors. The world is wealth as prison—everything looks right, nothing is.

Cinematography (9/10)

HBO production: Monterey photographed as paradise, the houses, the ocean, the school. The style is beautiful surfaces hiding ugly truths.

Visual Spectacle (4/10)

The spectacle is the wealth: the houses, the clothes, the lifestyle. The "spectacle" is the truth emerging—watching perfect lives shatter.

Conceptual Density (9/10)

Domestic violence, female friendship, protecting children, the lies we tell to keep up appearances, what happens when women support each other. The show explores these with depth.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

Someone died: the investigation reveals the secrets. Each episode is told through interview flash-forwards. The structure is mystery—who died, and why?

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Season 1 is complete perfection. Season 2 (with Meryl Streep) expands but feels unnecessary. The first season is the show. The death is revealed; the women unite.
