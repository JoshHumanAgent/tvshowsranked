Party of Five

1994 - 2000 • Fox • 142 Episodes

Score: 6.95
Drama, Family

What It Feels Like

The Salinger parents are killed by a drunk driver. Five children are left to raise themselves. Charlie, Bailey, Julia, Claudia, and baby Owen. 90s family drama: grief, growing up, and staying together. The show that launched stars.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Matthew Fox, Scott Wolf, Neve Campbell, Lacey Chabert. The ensemble carried the show through six seasons. The performances were the show's foundation.

World Building (6/10)

San Francisco: the restaurant, the house, the siblings' lives. The world is family tragedy made weekly.

Cinematography (5/10)

90s Fox production: the visual style of family drama. Functional and dated.

Visual Spectacle (1/10)

No spectacle. Family drama about grief and growing up.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Grief, responsibility, family as choice, growing up too fast. The show explored heavy themes for network TV.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Each sibling's journey; the family's survival. Family drama structure with real stakes.

Narrative Path & Resolution (7/10)

Six seasons, complete. The siblings grow up, move on. The ending earned the journey.
