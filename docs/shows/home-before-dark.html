What It Feels Like

Hilde Lisko is nine years old. She wants to be a journalist like her grandfather. When her family moves to a small town, she investigates a cold case that her father was involved in as a child. Inspired by real child journalist Hilde Lysiak. Apple TV+'s family mystery: young reporter, dark secrets.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Brooklynn Prince as Hilde: the nine-year-old journalist, determined and fearless. Jim Sturgess as her father, hiding secrets. The ensemble creates the small town. Prince is remarkable—young but commanding.

World Building (7/10)

Small town America: the newspaper office, the neighbors with secrets, the cold case that won't stay buried. The world is mystery-standard but family-accessible.

Cinematography (7/10)

Apple TV+ production: small-town America photographed with warmth and mystery, the past revealed through investigation. The style is family-friendly noir.

Visual Spectacle (2/10)

No spectacle. This is child investigation: interviews, research, discoveries. The "spectacle" is watching a nine-year-old solve what adults couldn't. Family mystery.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Truth-telling, generational secrets, journalism as service, what we hide to protect our families. The show explores these through a child's perspective. Age-appropriate depth.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Hilde investigates: each episode reveals more about the cold case, her father's involvement, the town's secrets. The structure is mystery—family-friendly pacing with real stakes.

Narrative Path & Resolution (6/10)

Two seasons. Season 1 resolves its case; season 2 continues Hilde's journalism. The show was designed as family viewing with mystery elements. Complete within seasons.
