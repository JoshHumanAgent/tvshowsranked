Schitt's Creek

2015 - 2020 • CBC/Pop • 80 Episodes

Score: 7.70
Comedy, Family

What It Feels Like

The Roses are rich, then they're not. They move to Schitt's Creek, a town they bought as a joke. Eugene Levy and Dan Levy (his son) created warm comedy about entitled people learning to care. The show that got better every season.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (10/10)

Eugene Levy as Johnny, Catherine O'Hara as Moira, Dan Levy as David, Annie Murphy as Alexis. The performances create entitlement and growth. Catherine O'Hara is legendary.

World Building (8/10)

Schitt's Creek: the motel, the town, the locals (Patrick, Twyla, Ronnie, Stevie). The world is small town as redemption.

Cinematography (6/10)

CBC production: the motel, the town, the visual language of Canadian sitcom. Functional but warm.

Visual Spectacle (1/10)

No spectacle. This is a family in a motel. The "spectacle" is watching terrible people become better.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Class, redemption, queer coming-out, family love, small-town value. The show explores these with warmth.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Each Rose learns to be human. David's romance with Patrick. Alexis's growth. The drive is character transformation.

Narrative Path & Resolution (10/10)

Six seasons, complete. The ending is perfect—the Roses leave but stay connected. The wedding is earned. Heartwarming finale.
