What It Feels Like to Watch

In the future, death is temporary. Human consciousness can be transferred between bodies—"sleeves"—allowing the rich to live forever. When a billionaire is murdered in his 300-year-old body, a soldier from the past is brought back to solve the case. Nothing is what it seems, and everyone has secrets.

Joel Kinnaman (again!) plays Takeshi Kovacs, a former Envoy soldier whose unique skills make him the only person capable of solving this crime. The world-building is extraordinary: neon-lit Bay City (a flooded San Francisco), orbital elevators, religious Meths who accumulate centuries of bodies. This is cyberpunk as only Netflix could afford.

The first season is the gold standard. It adapts Richard K. Morgan's novel with verve, updating the setting while maintaining the core themes. The plot is intricate, the characters are compelling, and the visual design is stunning. Later seasons recast and reinvent, but this is where it matters.

⚠️ Score reflects Season 1 only—the definitive version. Later seasons excluded per ranking guidelines.
