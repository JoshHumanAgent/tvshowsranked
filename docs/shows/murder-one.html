What It Feels Like to Watch

Before The Practice, before L.A. Law, there was Murder One. A law firm takes on a high-profile murder case, and the show follows both the trial and the personal lives of the attorneys. It was the first television series to make a season-long arc the backbone of a legal drama.

Anthony LaPaglia plays a defense attorney whose client is accused of killing a Hollywood actress. The case takes the entire season to resolve—radical at the time. The supporting cast includes a young Samuel L. Jackson and a pre-ER Anthony Edwards. It's '90s prestige television with all the style and substance that implies.

The show's innovation was in treating television like novels: long arcs, complex characters, consequences that rippled through episodes. It paved the way for everything that followed in television's golden age.
