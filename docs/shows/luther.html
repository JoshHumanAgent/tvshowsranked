Luther

2010 - 2019 • BBC • 21 Episodes

Score: 7.65
Crime, Thriller

What It Feels Like

DCI John Luther is obsessive, brilliant, and dangerous. He catches serial killers by thinking like them. Idris Elba is Luther: the detective as force of nature. Each season brings a new case, a new nemesis. Neil Cross created the detective as gothic hero.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Idris Elba as Luther: intensity, violence, moral complexity. Ruth Wilson as Alice Morgan: the psychopath who understands him. The guest villains (David Tennant, Paul McGann) bring the cases. Elba owns the role.

World Building (8/10)

London: the murders, the police stations, the darkness under the city. The world is gothic London—the city as labyrinth, the cases as descent.

Cinematography (8/10)

BBC production: London photographed for noir, the cases staged for horror. The style is gothic—the camera loves the darkness.

Visual Spectacle (5/10)

The spectacle is the cases: serial killers, elaborate murders, the darkness. The "spectacle" is Luther's intensity.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Obsession, the cost of justice, whether we can fight monsters without becoming them, the detective as destroyer. The show explores these through Luther's cases.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

Each season is a case: the killer, the hunt, the cost. The structure is procedural gothic—the drive is Luther's obsession.

Narrative Path & Resolution (7/10)

Five seasons plus film. Each season resolves its case; Luther's story continues. The film (2023) provided further continuation. The character endures.
