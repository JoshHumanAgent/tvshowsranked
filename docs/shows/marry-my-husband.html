Marry My Husband

2024 - Present • Korean Drama • Ongoing

Score: 7.25
Fantasy, Revenge

What It Feels Like

Kang Ji-won is murdered by her husband after discovering his affair with her best friend. She wakes up ten years earlier. Now she can change everything—and make them marry each other instead. Korean revenge fantasy: satisfying, cathartic, addictive.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Park Min-young as Ji-won: from victim to architect of revenge. The husband, the friend, the new love interest. Korean drama performances at the genre's best.

World Building (7/10)

Two timelines: the original tragedy and the second chance. The world is corporate Korea with supernatural intervention.

Cinematography (7/10)

Korean drama production: office politics, domestic spaces, the look of revenge satisfyingly executed.

Visual Spectacle (5/10)

The spectacle is emotional—revenge served perfectly. Moderate visual elements.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Second chances, revenge, taking control of your life. The show explores what we'd do with foresight.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

Change your fate; destroy those who hurt you. Each episode is revenge progression. Highly addictive.

Narrative Path & Resolution (7/10)

Season 1 complete. The revenge is satisfying. More potential story but first arc concludes.
