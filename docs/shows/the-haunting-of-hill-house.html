What It Feels Like to Watch

Mike Flanagan's Hill House is horror as family tragedy. The Crain family moves into a haunted house; years later, the trauma still festers. The show intercuts past and present, showing the children then and the damaged adults now.

The ghosts are everywhere—hidden in frames, lurking in backgrounds—rewards for attentive viewers. But the horror is grief: the mother's descent, the father's denial, the siblings' various dysfunctions.

The "bent-neck lady" reveal is among television's great horror twists. The long takes achieve technical virtuosity while maintaining dread.
