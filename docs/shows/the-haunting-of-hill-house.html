The Haunting of Hill House

2018 • Netflix • 10 Episodes

Score: 7.75
Horror, Drama

What It Feels Like

The Crain family lived in Hill House. It broke them. Now they're adults, still broken. One of them is dead. Mike Flanagan adapted Shirley Jackson: the house as wound, the family as haunting, grief made supernatural.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (10/10)

The Crain siblings: Michiel Huisman, Elizabeth Reaser, Oliver Jackson-Cohen, Kate Siegel, Victoria Pedretti. Carla Gugino as the mother. The performances capture trauma as haunting. Each sibling is a wound.

World Building (9/10)

Hill House: the rooms, the hallways, the basement, the red door. The house is character—every room is wound. The world is the house and the family, past and present.

Cinematography (9/10)

Netflix production: the house photographed for horror and beauty, the ghosts hidden in frames. The long takes are legendary. Flanagan's style is patient horror.

Visual Spectacle (8/10)

The spectacle is the house: the ghosts, the red room, the hauntings. The production design is meticulous—every frame has secrets.

Conceptual Density (9/10)

Grief as haunting, trauma as architecture, whether the house creates the wounds or the wounds create the house, what we pass to our children. The show explores family through horror.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

Past and present: what happened in the house, what's happening now. Each episode is a sibling's wound. The structure is trauma revealed—slowly, painfully.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Complete miniseries (anthology format). The Crain family story resolves. The house remains. One of the best horror series ever made.
