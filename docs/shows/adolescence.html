What It Feels Like to Watch

Netflix's Adolescence (2025) is a four-episode limited series told in real-time single takes—a 13-year-old boy accused of murder, his family, the investigation, and the aftermath. Each episode is one continuous shot, following the story through school, police station, home, and psychologist's office.

The format is the thesis: there's no escape from the camera, no cutting away, no relief. We watch a family's implosion in real time. The 13-year-old (newcomer Cooper Carter) is devastating—a child accused of adult violence, still a child.

Stephen Graham and Hannah Walters play the parents, their marriage fracturing under the weight of what their son might have done. The show asks: what makes a child kill? What does the internet teach boys about girls?
