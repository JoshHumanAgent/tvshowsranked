What It Feels Like to Watch

Mark Rylance as Thomas Cromwell—Henry VIII's fixer, rendered not as villain but as survivor. Hilary Mantel's novels adapted for television with quiet intensity. We watch Cromwell navigate Henry's divorce, Anne Boleyn's rise and fall, and the religious transformation of England.

Rylance's Cromwell is the show's achievement: a man who speaks softly, watches everything, remembers every slight. His stillness is the performance—when he finally moves, it's devastating. Damian Lewis's Henry is spoilt, dangerous, needy.

The politics are rendered as survival. Cromwell serves Henry while protecting himself, advancing his family, and settling scores. Anne Boleyn's fall is bureaucracy in motion—charges, confessions, executions.
