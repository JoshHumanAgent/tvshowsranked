Wolf Hall

2015 - 2024 • BBC • 12 Episodes

Score: 7.50
Historical Drama

What It Feels Like

Thomas Cromwell rises from blacksmith's son to Henry VIII's most trusted advisor. He engineers the break with Rome, the dissolution of the monasteries, Anne Boleyn's fall. Mark Rylance is Cromwell: silent, watching, lethal. Hilary Mantel's novels adapted by Peter Straughan: Tudor politics as chess, power as survival.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (10/10)

Mark Rylance as Cromwell: one of television's great performances. Stillness as power, withholding as threat. Damian Lewis as Henry VIII: the king whose whims determine fates. Claire Foy as Anne Boleyn: the queen who overreaches. The ensemble is Shakespearean quality.

World Building (10/10)

Tudor England: the court, the legal system, the religious houses, the European politics. The world is complete—Mantel's research translated to screen. Every detail is specific and authentic.

Cinematography (9/10)

BBC prestige production: the court rendered with candlelit authenticity, the clothes perfect, the locations real. The style is observation—the camera watches Cromwell watch others. Period filmmaking at its finest.

Visual Spectacle (6/10)

The spectacle is period: the court, the ceremonies, the executions. The spectacle serves the history—not battlefields but council chambers. The spectacle is watching power operate.

Conceptual Density (10/10)

Power, survival, the cost of competence, what we owe to our pasts, the religious reformation as political necessity. The show explores Tudor politics with Mantel's depth. Dense and rewarding.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Cromwell must navigate Henry's desires, Anne's ambition, the Pope's refusal, the nobles' contempt. The drive is political survival—each decision has consequences. The pacing is deliberate but propulsive.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Season 1 covers Cromwell's rise and Anne's fall. Season 2 (2024) covers the end of his life. The ending is historical—Cromwell's fall is known. The drama is in the how, not the what. Masterful.
