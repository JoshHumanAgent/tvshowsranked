What It Feels Like to Watch

Sam Esmail's Mr. Robot is Fight Club for hackers—unreliable narrator, corporate rebellion, identity fragmentation. Rami Malek's Elliot is a cybersecurity engineer by day, vigilante hacker by night, unraveling mentally while trying to dismantle the conglomerate that owns everything.

The show's visual language is distinctive—framing characters in corners, leaving empty space, creating alienation. The hacking is (mostly) accurate. The corporate critique is sincere.

Four seasons of mental health exploration, with a finale that provides genuine closure.
