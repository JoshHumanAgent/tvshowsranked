The White Princess

2017 • Starz • 8 Episodes

Score: 7.15
Historical Drama

What It Feels Like

Henry Tudor defeated Richard III at Bosworth. He marries Elizabeth of York to unite the warring houses. But is their marriage a union or a prison? Philippa Gregory's novel adapted for Starz: the aftermath of the Wars of the Roses, told through the women who survived.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Jodie Comer as Elizabeth of York: caught between her Yorkist family and her Tudor husband. Jacob Collins-Levy as Henry VII: the victor uncertain of his throne. Michelle Fairley as Margaret Beaufort: the king's mother, terrifying in devotion. The performances capture the period tension.

World Building (8/10)

Post-Bosworth England: the Tudor claim fragile, the Yorkist pretenders threatening, the queen caught between. The world is 15th-century political marriage—intimate and dangerous.

Cinematography (7/10)

Starz production: Tudor England rendered with period detail, the marriage intimate, the politics claustrophobic. The style serves the drama—close quarters, whispered secrets.

Visual Spectacle (5/10)

The spectacle is period: the court, the ceremonies, the occasional battle. This is political drama—not battles but marriages, negotiations, threats. Intimate historical.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Political marriage vs. love, duty vs. family, what women owed to their houses vs. themselves, the cost of unifying enemies. The show explores these through Elizabeth's position.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

The pretenders threaten Henry's throne; Elizabeth must choose between her Yorkist family and her Tudor husband. The structure is political thriller—loyalty constantly tested.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Complete miniseries. The pretender threat resolves; the marriage settles; the Tudor dynasty is secure. The ending provides historical closure while leaving emotional complexity.
