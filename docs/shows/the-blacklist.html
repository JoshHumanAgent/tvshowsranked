The Blacklist

2013 - 2023 • NBC • 218 Episodes

Score: 7.15
Crime, Thriller

What It Feels Like

Raymond "Red" Reddington turns himself into the FBI. He has a list of criminals the FBI doesn't know about. He'll help catch them, but only if he works with profiler Elizabeth Keen. James Spader is Red: charming, dangerous, always five steps ahead. Ten seasons of criminal mastermind as consultant.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

James Spader is the show: the voice, the charm, the menace, the mystery. Megan Boone as Elizabeth Keen provides the FBI perspective. The guest criminals create variety. Spader's Reddington is television's most watchable mastermind.

World Building (7/10)

The criminal underworld: the blacklist, the syndicates, the shadow organizations. The world expands across ten seasons—the Cabal, the Syndicate, Red's empire. The mythology becomes complex.

Cinematography (6/10)

Network production: the FBI, the criminal operations, the international locations. The style is functional—Spader is the visual focus. The production serves the star.

Visual Spectacle (5/10)

The spectacle is criminal operations: heists, assassinations, escapes. The blacklisters provide variety—each episode has a new criminal. Network TV action, not cinematic.

Conceptual Density (6/10)

Trust and manipulation, identity, whether people can change, the cost of secrets. The show explores these through Red's relationship with Elizabeth. The themes are present; the plot dominates.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Blacklister of the week with serialized mystery: who is Red really? What's his connection to Elizabeth? The structure provides procedural reliability and serialized stakes. Spader keeps every episode watchable.

Narrative Path & Resolution (7/10)

Ten seasons with conclusion. The Red-Elizabeth mystery resolved (controversially). The ending provided answers to decade-long questions. Complete, though opinions on the conclusion vary.
