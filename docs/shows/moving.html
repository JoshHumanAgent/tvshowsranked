Moving

2023 • Disney+ • 20 Episodes

Score: 7.18
Superhero, K-Drama

What It Feels Like

Parents with superpowers hide their abilities. Their children inherit powers they don't understand. The parents were black ops agents; now they're being hunted. Park Hoon-jung adapts Kang Full's webtoon: Korean superhero drama as family story. The budget is massive, the emotion is real, the action is stunning.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Ryu Seung-ryong, Han Hyo-joo, Zo In-sung—the parents carry the past. The younger cast (Lee Jung-ha, Go Yoon-jung, Kim Do-hoon) brings the discovery. The performances ground the superpowers in family emotion. Korean drama acting at its finest.

World Building (9/10)

South Korea's hidden super-powered: the black ops program, the retired agents, the children discovering abilities. The world expands across episodes—Korean intelligence, North Korean operatives, American involvement. Complete and specific.

Cinematography (9/10)

Disney+ K-drama budget: the powers are rendered beautifully, the action is staged with precision, the emotional scenes are filmed with care. The style is Korean cinema elevated to television scale.

Visual Spectacle (9/10)

Superpowers: flight, regeneration, electrical control, super strength. The action sequences are stunning—the sky battle, the school fight, the apartment confrontation. Some of the best superhero action on television.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Parental sacrifice, inherited trauma, whether we protect our children by hiding the truth or preparing them, government use of individuals. The show explores superhero tropes through Korean family dynamics.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

The past catches up: the parents' histories are revealed, the children's powers emerge, the hunters arrive. The structure moves between timelines—past and present weaving together. The pacing is masterful.

Narrative Path & Resolution (9/10)

Complete miniseries. The story resolves—past and present collide, protect and prepared converge. The ending provides closure while leaving room for future stories. Satisfying.
