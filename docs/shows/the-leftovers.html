What It Feels Like to Watch

The Leftovers begins with an impossible premise: two percent of the world's population disappears instantly, with no explanation, no pattern, no warning. The Sudden Departure is a Rapture without religion, a tragedy without meaning. The series follows the survivors in a small New York town, three years after the event, as they try to live in a world where mystery has become the only constant.

Kevin Garvey (Justin Theroux) is the chief of police, trying to hold his family together while his wife (Carrie Coon) has joined a silent cult called the Guilty Remnant, his son has run away to follow a faith healer, and his daughter is withdrawing into teenage rebellion. The Guilty Remnant—people who've abandoned their old lives to wear white, smoke cigarettes, and silently remind everyone that nothing matters—loom over the series, a reminder that grief takes many forms.

Based on Tom Perrotta's novel but transcending it completely, The Leftovers is about what happens when you can't make sense of suffering. Some characters find faith; some lose it; some invent new religions; some simply refuse to accept the premise. The show takes risks that no other series would attempt: an episode from the perspective of a ghost, an episode set entirely in a single location, an entire season relocated to Australia for reasons that become increasingly clear.

The finale is among television's finest. Kevin and Nora find each other in Australia, years after separating, and tell each other stories. Nora claims she crossed to the other side and found a world where the departed never left. Kevin claims he died and came back. "I believe you," she says. "I believe you too," he answers. It doesn't matter if either story is true. They've found each other. The mystery remains, and they've learned to live with it.

Resolution (10): "I believe you." The perfect ending to a show about faith, grief, and learning to live with mystery.

Conceptual Density (10): The Sudden Departure, the Guilty Remnant, faith healers, cults, parallel universes—all in service of deep questions about meaning.

Characters & Acting (10): Carrie Coon and Justin Theroux give career-best performances. The supporting cast is equally powerful.

Key Episodes
