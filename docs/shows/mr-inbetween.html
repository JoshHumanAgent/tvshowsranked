Mr Inbetween

2018 - 2021 • FX • 26 Episodes

Score: 7.50
Crime, Drama

What It Feels Like

Ray Shoesmith is a hitman. He's also a father, a brother, a friend. He tries to balance work and life in suburban Sydney. Scott Ryan created and stars: the mundane violence, the dark comedy, the human hitman. Like Barry but Australian, darker, more human.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Scott Ryan as Ray: the hitman you root for, violent and vulnerable. The supporting cast creates his world—daughter, brother, friends, targets. Ryan's performance is lived-in, real.

World Building (8/10)

Suburban Sydney: the houses, the schools, the everyday places where Ray conducts his violence. The world is normal—Ray's work is the anomaly. Australian crime minimalism.

Cinematography (7/10)

FX production: handheld, observational, the violence sudden and brutal. The style is invisible—this is character, not spectacle. The 22-minute episodes are perfect.

Visual Spectacle (4/10)

The spectacle is violence: quick, brutal, matter-of-fact. Ray's work is choreographed for reality, not excitement. The "spectacle" is the contrast between work and life.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Work-life balance for monsters, whether violence can be compartmentalized, what we owe to family vs. work, the human face of killers. The show explores these through dark comedy.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Ray's work, Ray's family, Ray's attempts to be both. The structure is slice-of-life crime—episodes are 22 minutes of work-life balance for a hitman. The drive is Ray's humanity.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Three seasons, complete. Ray's story concludes with appropriate darkness. The ending is inevitable but earned. 26 episodes of perfect crime comedy-drama.
