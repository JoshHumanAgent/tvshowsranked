Broadchurch

2013 - 2017 • ITV • 24 Episodes

Score: 7.85
Crime, Drama

What It Feels Like

A boy is murdered in a small coastal town. Everyone is a suspect. David Tennant is the damaged detective; Olivia Colman is the local who knows everyone. Chris Chibnall's mystery: the community tearing apart, the secrets revealed, the coast as Greek chorus.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (10/10)

David Tennant as Alec Hardy: the outsider with secrets. Olivia Colman as Ellie Miller: the local whose life is destroyed. Jodie Whittaker, Andrew Buchan, Arthur Darvill—the town is fully cast. The performances are British drama at its best.

World Building (9/10)

Broadchurch, Dorset: the cliffs, the beach, the community. The world is small-town Britain—everyone knows everyone, everyone has secrets. The location is character.

Cinematography (9/10)

ITV production: the Dorset coast photographed with gothic beauty, the cliffs as omen. The style is observational—watching a community fall apart.

Visual Spectacle (3/10)

No spectacle. This is murder investigation: interviews, secrets, the town unraveling. The "spectacle" is emotional—watching a community confront what it's hiding.

Conceptual Density (9/10)

Grief, community, whether we can ever know anyone, the cost of secrets, how murder destroys more than the victim. The show explores these with depth.

Narrative Drive (10/10)

Who killed the boy? Each episode reveals more secrets, narrows suspects, deepens the tragedy. The structure is mystery—impossible to stop watching.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Three seasons. Season 1 is the boy's murder—perfect. Season 2 is the trial—divisive but complete. Season 3 is a new case—strong. The first season is the classic.
