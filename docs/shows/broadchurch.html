What It Feels Like to Watch

Broadchurch is what happens when a murder mystery takes place in a small town where everyone knows everyone. A boy is found dead on the beach. Two detectives with very different styles must find his killer. But this isn't about the puzzle—it's about what a murder does to a community that thought it was safe.

David Tennant plays DI Alec Hardy, a damaged big-city detective transferred to the coast after a failed case. Olivia Colman plays DS Ellie Miller, a local who knows everyone, whose son was the victim's best friend. Their partnership is the show's engine: Hardy is abrasive and hiding something; Miller is warm and shattered by a crime she never expected in her town.

The Dorset coast is rendered in golden light and stark cliffs—the show is beautiful even as it's devastating. The town itself becomes a character: the newsagent, the priest, the hotel owner, the teacher. Everyone has secrets. Everyone is a suspect. The whodunit structure keeps you guessing, but the real story is what suspicion does to relationships.

The first season is a complete story—the investigation of Danny Latimer's death. The second season deals with the trial and the aftermath. The third season jumps forward years to a new case—a sexual assault that exposes different fissures in the community. Each season uses crime to explore something larger: grief, justice, trauma.

Olivia Colman's performance is the heart. Her Miller is decent, human, trying to hold it together when everything she believed about her town and her life falls apart. When she finally learns the truth about the killer, her reaction is devastating because it's so real—no histrionics, just a woman broken by betrayal.

Characters & Acting (9): David Tennant and Olivia Colman are perfect together. The supporting cast—Jodie Whittaker, Andrew Buchan, Arthur Darvill—fills out a community that feels real.

World Building (9): Broadchurch exists. The town, the beach, the cliffs, the relationships—all of it breathes. You understand why people stay even after tragedy.

Resolution (8): The first season's reveal is earned. The subsequent seasons build on rather than diminish the original story. The trilogy completes satisfactorily.
