What It Feels Like to Watch

The bombs fell in 2077. Two hundred years later, the survivors—those who hid in vaults and those who didn't—emerge into a wasteland of radiation, mutants, and factions fighting over scraps. This is Fallout: part satire, part horror, part Western.

Ella Purnell's Lucy is a vault dweller who leaves her underground paradise to save her father. Walton Goggins' Ghoul is a bounty hunter who's seen it all. Aaron Moten's Maximus is a squire in the Brotherhood of Steel, desperate for purpose. Their stories intertwine across the blasted remains of Los Angeles.

The show captures the game's DNA: the dark humor, the 1950s aesthetics, the moral ambiguity of the factions. The production design is meticulous—every vault, every power armor suit, every irradiated creature looks like it stepped out of the games. It's the best video game adaptation since Arcane.
