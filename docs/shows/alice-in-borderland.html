What It Feels Like to Watch

Arisu (Kento Yamazaki) is a video game-obsessed unemployed man-child. After an accident, he wakes up in a version of Tokyo that's completely empty—except for other survivors. Then the games begin. Win, you live. Lose, you die. The games range from simple tests of strength to brutal psychological experiments.

Based on Haro Aso's manga, Alice in Borderland is survival horror with a puzzle-box structure. Each game has rules that must be understood and exploited. The players are forced to confront their own mortality, their own morality, and their own capacity for violence.

The production values are extraordinary—Sato's direction is cinematic, the sets are massive, the action is visceral. But the show's heart is its characters: Arisu, Usagi (Tao Tsuchiya), Chishiya (Nijirô Murakami), and the others who navigate this hellscape.
