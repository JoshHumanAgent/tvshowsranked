Master of None

2015 - 2021 • Netflix • 25 Episodes

Score: 7.30
Comedy, Drama

What It Feels Like

Aziz Ansari is Dev, an actor in New York. He dates, he acts, he negotiates with his parents. Season 2 has an episode in Italy. Season 3 is about Denise and Alicia. Aziz Ansari and Alan Yang's anthology comedy: identity, relationships, immigrant parents, pasta in Italy.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Aziz Ansari as Dev: a version of himself. His parents play themselves. Lena Waithe as Denise. Naomi Ackie in season 3. The performances are naturalistic comedy.

World Building (7/10)

New New York, Modena Italy, the immigrant experience. Season 3 shifts to different characters entirely—same world, different perspective.

Cinematography (8/10)

Netflix production: "New York, I Love You" and "Thanksgiving" (Waithe's Emmy-winning episode) are visual standouts. The Italian season is beautiful.

Visual Spectacle (3/10)

The spectacle is Italy: pasta making, scooter rides. But mostly this is people talking in places.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Immigrant children, dating apps, commitment, queer identity, creative fulfillment. The show explores identity with millennial precision.

Narrative Drive (6/10)

Episodic and thematic. Some episodes are experimental ("New York, I Love You"). The drive is character insight.

Narrative Path & Resolution (5/10)

Anthology structure. Dev's story arcs incompletely; season 3 is different characters. The show evolves unpredictably.
