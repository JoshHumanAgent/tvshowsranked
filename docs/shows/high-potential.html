What It Feels Like

Morgan is a single mom with high potential—she's a genius, but her life is chaos. She works as a cleaner at the police station. When she rearranges evidence during a cleaning shift and solves a case, she's hired as a consultant. Kaitlin Olson stars in the adaptation of the French series HPI: the chaotic genius who sees what others miss.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Kaitlin Olson is Morgan: chaotic, brilliant, struggling to hold her life together while solving crimes. The contrast from It's Always Sunny shows range. The supporting cast provides the procedural framework. Olson carries the show.

World Building (6/10)

The Los Angeles police station, Morgan's chaotic home, the cases of the week. The world is functional—procedural setting with a genius consultant overlay. The genius tropes are familiar.

Cinematography (5/10)

Network procedural aesthetics: bright, functional, designed for syndication. The style is invisible—this is comfort television with a charismatic lead. The cases are staged clearly.

Visual Spectacle (2/10)

No spectacle. This is procedural crime-solving: evidence, deduction, case closed. The "spectacle" is watching Morgan's chaotic mind work. Comfort television.

Conceptual Density (5/10)

Genius vs. normal, whether potential equals success, the chaos of exceptional minds. The themes are present but secondary to the procedural. Light drama with character work.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Case of the week with serialized character development: Morgan's parenting, her romantic life, her career. The structure is procedural reliable—each case resolves, the character grows.

Narrative Path & Resolution (4/10)

Ongoing. Season 1 airing. The procedural formula is established; the character arcs are developing. Too early to assess conclusion.
