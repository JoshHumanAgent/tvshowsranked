What It Feels Like to Watch

Yellowjackets is what happens when Lord of the Flies meets Lost with a feminist lens. A high school girls soccer team's plane crashes in the Canadian wilderness. They survive for nineteen months. Some of them make it home. What they did to survive becomes the show's central mystery—a secret they've kept for twenty-five years.

The show splits between two timelines. In 1996, the team deals with the crash, the wilderness, and the gradual breakdown of civilization. Shauna (Sophie Nélisse) is pregnant with her best friend's boyfriend's baby. Jackie (Ella Purnell) is the team captain who can't accept that she's not in control. Misty (Samantha Hanratty) is the team manager who finds purpose—maybe too much purpose—in being needed. Taissa (Jasmin Savoy Brown) sleepwalks into danger. Lottie (Courtney Eaton) starts having visions.

In 2021, the survivors are adults with secrets. Shauna (Melanie Lynskey) is in an unhappy marriage, still carrying the guilt of what happened. Taissa (Tawny Cypress) is running for office while hiding her sleepwalking from her wife. Natalie (Juliette Lewis) is in and out of rehab, still chasing the highs. Misty (Christina Ricci) is a nurse who may or may not be killing patients. Someone is blackmailing them, and the past won't stay buried.

The show's genius is the dual timeline. We know some survive, some don't, but the show keeps us guessing about who and how. The wilderness becomes a character—beautiful, hostile, possibly supernatural. Ritual emerges. Violence becomes necessary. The girls build something that helps them survive and destroys who they were.

Christina Ricci's adult Misty is the standout—friendly, helpful, terrifying. She knows what happened, she knows what she did, and she's fine with it.
