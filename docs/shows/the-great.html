The Great

2020 - 2023 • Hulu • 30 Episodes

Score: 7.18
Historical Comedy, Drama

What It Feels Like

Catherine arrives in Russia to marry Emperor Peter. He's an idiot. She decides to kill him and take the throne. Elle Fanning is Catherine; Nicholas Hoult is Peter. Tony McNamara created "an occasionally true story": historical farce as political satire, 18th-century Russia rendered as absurdist comedy.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Elle Fanning as Catherine: the idealist who becomes ruthless. Nicholas Hoult as Peter: the idiot king who becomes almost sympathetic. The supporting cast (Phoebe Fox, Sacha Dhawan, Charity Wakefield) creates the absurd court. The performances are theatrical and perfect.

World Building (9/10)

18th-century Russia as playground: the court, the politics, the violence, the absurdity. The world is stylized history—real enough to matter, absurd enough to work. The aesthetic is distinctive.

Cinematography (8/10)

Hulu production: sumptuous costumes, palace interiors, the Russian winter. The style is theatrical—the camera observes the absurdity. Beautiful and biting.

Visual Spectacle (7/10)

The spectacle is the court: the costumes, the ceremonies, the occasional violence. The production is lavish; the tone is satirical. The spectacle serves the comedy.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Enlightenment idealism vs. realpolitik, whether ideals survive power, the cost of revolution, men in power vs. women who want it. The show explores these through comedy that cuts.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Catherine's coup: each season is a phase of her plot. The structure is farce with stakes—will she kill him, will she succeed, what will power cost? The drive is constant.

Narrative Path & Resolution (7/10)

Three seasons, cancelled. Catherine takes power; the story was continuing. The cancellation left threads unresolved. The journey is excellent; the destination is incomplete.
