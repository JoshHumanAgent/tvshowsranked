Traces

2019 - 2022 • Channel 4 • 12 Episodes

Score: 7.15
Crime, Mystery

What It Feels Like

Emma Hedges works as a lab assistant at the Scottish Institute of Forensic Science. When an online course recreates her mother's murder, she investigates. Three women at the institute—Emma, a professor, and a detective—become involved in separate cases. Scottish crime drama with forensic science focus.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (7/10)

Molly Windsor as Emma: the young woman investigating her mother's death. Laura Fraser as the professor, Jennifer Spence as the detective. The three women create the center. The performances are BBC solid.

World Building (7/10)

Dundee, Scotland: the forensics institute, the police, the cases. The world is specific to forensic science—the lab is the setting. Scottish crime with technical detail.

Cinematography (6/10)

Channel 4 production: Dundee photographed grey and Scottish, the lab rendered with detail. The style is functional—crime drama aesthetics without distinction.

Visual Spectacle (2/10)

No spectacle. This is forensic science: labs, evidence, investigation. The "spectacle" is the science—watching evidence analyzed. British crime procedural.

Conceptual Density (6/10)

Forensic science, cold cases, women in science, personal vs. professional. The show explores these through the cases. The forensic focus distinguishes it.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Emma's mother's murder, the professor's case, the detective's investigation. Three threads that eventually connect. The structure is mystery driven by multiple cases.

Narrative Path & Resolution (6/10)

Two seasons. The first season resolves Emma's mother's case; the second continues with new cases. The show wasn't designed for long running. Complete enough.
