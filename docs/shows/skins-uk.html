Skins (UK)

2007 - 2013 • E4 • 61 Episodes

Score: 7.45
Drama, Teen

What It Feels Like

Bristol teens party, fuck up, and try to survive. Each generation brings new faces. Effy is the throughline. The show that made teen drama raw: drugs, eating disorders, mental illness, family—nothing softened. British youth as it actually was.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

The casts rotated every two seasons: Nicholas Hoult, Kaya Scodelario, Hannah Murray, Dev Patel. Each generation had its standouts. Effy is iconic.

World Building (8/10)

Bristol: the parties, the homes, the schools. Working-class teen life rendered authentically. The world is specific to British youth culture.

Cinematography (7/10)

E4 production: handheld, party scenes, the visual language of teen drama. The style matches the chaos of youth.

Visual Spectacle (3/10)

No spectacle. Teen life: parties, bedrooms, streets. The "spectacle" is how raw it gets.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Teen mental health, addiction, sexuality, family dysfunction, friendship as survival. Gen Z before Gen Z had a name.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Each episode focuses on one character. The structure lets each teen have their story. The drive is character exploration.

Narrative Path & Resolution (7/10)

Seven seasons, complete. Each generation got resolution. Effy's story concluded in "Skins: Rise." A defining show of its era.
