Reservation Dogs

2021 - 2023 • FX • 28 Episodes

Score: 7.90
Comedy, Drama

What It Feels Like

Four Indigenous teenagers in rural Oklahoma steal, scheme, and dream of California. They're mourning their friend who died. Sterlin Harjo and Taika Waititi created the show: Indigenous writers, Indigenous cast, Indigenous stories. Comedy with profound grief underneath.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

The ensemble of young actors (Devery Jacobs, D'Pharaoh Woon-A-Tai, Paulina Alexis, Lane Factor) creates the friend group. The community around them adds depth. The performances are naturalistic and specific.

World Building (10/10)

Rural Oklahoma reservation: the community, the struggles, the humor, the history. The world is specific to Indigenous experience—never condescending, always real. One of television's most authentic depictions.

Cinematography (8/10)

FX production: rural Oklahoma photographed with affection, the community rendered in detail. The style is observational—this is lived-in, not poverty tourism.

Visual Spectacle (2/10)

No spectacle. This is life: schemes, community, grief, humor. The "spectacle" is the specificity—seeing Indigenous life on television without stereotype.

Conceptual Density (9/10)

Grief, community, what we inherit, what we leave, Indigenous identity in modern America, the dream of escape vs. the value of home. The show explores these through comedy that deepens.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

California is the dream: they steal to get money for the trip, they scheme, they plan. The structure is coming-of-age—the drive is adulthood approaching.

Narrative Path & Resolution (9/10)

Three seasons, complete. The story concludes with characters moving forward. Indigenous storytelling with proper ending. Essential television.
