What It Feels Like to Watch

Ray McKinnon's Rectify is television as meditation—Daniel Holden released from death row after 19 years, returning to a town that still believes he's guilty. Aden Young's Daniel is otherworldly, traumatized, innocent maybe. Sixteen years on death row have made him strange to the world and himself.

The show is glacial, deliberate, beautiful. Time moves differently. Every moment is stretched. The question of guilt is secondary to the question of reintegration.
