The White Queen

2013 • Starz/BBC • 10 Episodes

Score: 7.18
Historical Drama

What It Feels Like

Elizabeth Woodville marries Edward IV in secret. She becomes queen. The Wars of the Roses engulf England. Philippa Gregory's novel adapted for television: the conflict through three women—Elizabeth, Margaret Beaufort, Anne Neville. History told through the women who shaped it.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Rebecca Ferguson as Elizabeth Woodville: the commoner who becomes queen. Amanda Hale as Margaret Beaufort: the Lancastrian who will wait decades for her son. Faye Marsay as Anne Neville: the pawn who becomes player. The performances are stellar.

World Building (9/10)

Wars of the Roses England: the courts, the battles, the marriages that determine kingdoms. The world is complete—15th century politics through women's eyes. The historical detail immerses.

Cinematography (7/10)

Starz/BBC co-production: period detail, the castles, the battles rendered with budget. The style is historical drama—sumptuous but focused on character.

Visual Spectacle (6/10)

The spectacle is period: the battles, the coronations, the court. The production value serves the history. Not epic spectacle but historical drama.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Women in history, marriage as politics, survival in a world of changing fortunes, what we sacrifice for our children. The show explores these through three very different women.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Elizabeth's rise, Margaret's patient plotting, Anne's journey from pawn to player. The structure is triptych—three stories converging on Bosworth. The drive is historical momentum.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Complete miniseries. The Wars of the Roses end at Bosworth; the Tudor dynasty begins. The ending satisfies historically and emotionally. The White Princess continues the story.
