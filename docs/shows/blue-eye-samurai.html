Blue Eye Samurai

2023 - Present • Netflix • Ongoing

Score: 7.85
Animation, Action

What It Feels Like

Mizu is a mixed-race woman in Edo-period Japan. Blue eyes mark her as outsider. She seeks revenge on the white men who fathered her. She becomes a swordmaster. Animation as revenge Western: beautifully violent, deeply personal.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Mizu is the warrior: driven, skilled, broken. The supporting cast creates Edo Japan. The voice acting captures the fury and the stillness.

World Building (10/10)

Edo Japan: the shogunate, the foreigners, the sword schools, the class system. The world is rendered with detail—every frame is Japan with precision.

Cinematography (10/10)

Netflix animation: the sword fights staged like ballet, the snow, the blood. The style is anime-influenced Western animation. The action is breathtaking.

Visual Spectacle (10/10)

The spectacle is the sword work: every fight is choreographed masterpiece. The animation allows camera moves live-action can't match. The action is art.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Revenge, identity, race in homogeneous society, what we sacrifice for vengeance, being outsider. The show explores these through the revenge plot.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

Find the white men. Kill them. Each episode brings Mizu closer. The structure is revenge Western—relentless, violent, personal.

Narrative Path & Resolution (6/10)

Ongoing. Season 1 advances the revenge; more seasons planned. The story is incomplete but the journey is compelling.
