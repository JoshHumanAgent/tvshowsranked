Lioness

2023 - Present • Paramount+ • Ongoing

Score: 7.15
Spy, Thriller

What It Feels Like

The CIA's Lioness program infiltrates terrorist networks through women close to targets. Cruz Manuelos is recruited into the program. Zoe Saldaña is Joe, the station chief; Nicole Kidman plays her supervisor. Taylor Sheridan creates spy thriller as Taylor Sheridan: personal cost, professional stakes, Montana values.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Zoe Saldaña as Joe: the operative managing field work and family. Laysla De Oliveira as Cruz: the recruit in danger. Nicole Kidman, Morgan Freeman add star power. The performances are committed and physical.

World Building (8/10)

CIA operations, terrorist networks, the infiltration programs. The world expands across borders—training facilities, target locations, family homes. Sheridan's spy world is personalized.

Cinematography (8/10)

Paramount+ production with Sheridan style: military precision, personal stakes, action staged for impact. The style balances spy thriller with family drama.

Visual Spectacle (7/10)

The spectacle is spy action: training, infiltration, extraction. The action is staged with budget and precision. Sheridan knows how to shoot tension.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Personal cost of service, family vs. mission, the women who do the work men can't, loyalty and betrayal. The show explores these through Cruz's infiltration and Joe's management.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

The mission: infiltrate, befriend, enable the kill. Each episode brings Cruz closer to the target and deeper into danger. The structure is mission-based with personal cost.

Narrative Path & Resolution (5/10)

Ongoing. Two seasons completed. The program continues; new Lionesses are recruited. No endpoint announced—Sheridan shows tend to run.
