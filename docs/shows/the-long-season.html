The Long Season

2022 • iQIYI • 12 Episodes

Score: 7.20
Chinese Drama, Mystery

What It Feels Like

A murder in a small Northeast China town connects three timelines: 1990, 1998, and 2016. The same actors play different ages across decades. The case haunts everyone involved. Chinese drama as literary mystery: time, memory, and a northern industrial town in decline.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

The ensemble plays across decades: aging, regret, the weight of the unsolved case. The performances are Chinese drama at its finest—subtle, cumulative, devastating. The aging makeup and performance capture different lives.

World Building (9/10)

Northeast China across three decades: the industrial boom, the decline, the present emptiness. The town is character—changing with time, holding the murder in its memory. Specific and atmospheric.

Cinematography (8/10)

Chinese prestige drama production: the northern town rendered in three eras, the snow, the factories, the decline. The style is literary—the camera observes time passing.

Visual Spectacle (4/10)

The spectacle is atmospheric: the snow, the factories, the three timelines visually distinguished. This is memory mystery—not spectacle but texture.

Conceptual Density (9/10)

Time, memory, how the past shapes the present, industrial decline, what we lose when towns die, whether cases can be solved after decades. The show explores these through literary structure.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Three timelines converge on the murder: what happened, how it shaped everyone, whether it can be solved. The structure is puzzle—past and present revealing each other. The pacing is deliberate.

Narrative Path & Resolution (9/10)

Complete miniseries. The case is solved; the timelines converge; the weight lifts. The ending provides resolution and emotional payoff. Chinese prestige drama with proper conclusion.
