Professor T

2021 - Present • ITV • Ongoing

Score: 7.15
Crime, Procedural

What It Feels Like

Professor Jasper Tempest is a brilliant criminology professor at Cambridge. He consults for the police. He's also obsessive-compulsive, germaphobic, and socially difficult. Ben Miller is Professor T in the English remake of the Belgian series: Monk meets academia, with an English accent.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (7/10)

Ben Miller as Professor T: the brilliant but difficult consultant. The police team provides the cases and the friction. The performances are procedural solid—Miller is charming despite the character's difficulties.

World Building (6/10)

Cambridge University and the police: the academia, the crime scenes, the professor's routines. The world serves the procedural—distinctive mainly for the Cambridge setting.

Cinematography (6/10)

ITV production: Cambridge photographed nicely, the crime scenes procedural-standard. The style is functional—this is comfort crime TV.

Visual Spectacle (2/10)

No spectacle. This is procedural crime: cases, deductions, resolutions. The "spectacle" is Professor T's eccentricities and insights. Classic detective TV.

Conceptual Density (5/10)

Brilliance and difficulty, the cost of genius, whether we accept people as they are. The themes are present in the character; the procedural is the focus. Light exploration.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Case of the week with serialized character development: Professor T's therapy, his relationship with the police. The structure is reliable procedural—each case solves, the character grows.

Narrative Path & Resolution (5/10)

Ongoing. Three seasons completed. The procedural formula continues; the character arc progresses slowly. No endpoint announced.
