Yellowstone

2018 - 2024 • Paramount • 53 Episodes

Score: 7.40
Western, Drama

What It Feels Like

John Dutton owns the largest ranch in Montana. Developers want it. His children fight over it. Kevin Costner is Dutton: the patriarch who will do anything to protect his land. Taylor Sheridan created the modern Western: succession in the American West.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Kevin Costner as John Dutton: the patriarch, the protector, the immovable object. Luke Grimes, Kelly Reilly, Wes Bentley as the children. The performances embrace the Western genre.

World Building (9/10)

Montana ranching: the reservation, the developers, the land, the politics. The world is the American West as battleground—ranching vs. development vs. tribal sovereignty.

Cinematography (9/10)

Paramount production: Montana photographed with grandeur, the ranch as kingdom, the West as character. The style is epic—the land is the point.

Visual Spectacle (8/10)

The spectacle is the land: the mountains, the cattle, the ranch. This is Western cinema on television budget. The landscape is the star.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Land ownership, what we inherit, the West as myth and reality, development vs. preservation, what fathers owe children. The show explores through family drama.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Protect the ranch. Each season brings new threats: developers, politicians, family. The structure is Western siege—the land must be defended.

Narrative Path & Resolution (6/10)

Five partial seasons. Costner left; the ending is in production. The show continues with the Dutton saga. The resolution is pending.
