Dark

2017 - 2020 • Netflix • 26 Episodes

Score: 8.35
Sci-Fi, Mystery

What It Feels Like

A child disappears in a small German town. Then another. The investigation reveals a time travel conspiracy spanning generations. Dark is Netflix's first German original: complex, philosophical, time travel as destiny and doom.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Louis Hofmann, Maja Schöne, Oliver Masucci—the cast plays multiple versions across time. The performances hold the complexity together. The transitions between ages are seamless.

World Building (10/10)

Winden: the nuclear plant, the caves, the 33-year cycle. The world is built with scientific precision—time travel has rules, and every trip has consequences. Complete mythology.

Cinematography (9/10)

Netflix production: the German forest, the rain, the 1950s/1980s/2040s rendered in distinct palettes. The style is ominous—this is time travel as horror.

Visual Spectacle (6/10)

The spectacle is the caves, the time machine, the apocalyptic future. The production visualizes temporal mechanics with clarity.

Conceptual Density (10/10)

Time, determinism, whether we can change fate, the bootstrap paradox, generational trauma. The show explores these with philosophical depth. Requires attention.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

The mystery unfolds across three seasons—who is the father, the killer, the origin. The structure is puzzle—the drive is understanding. Each season deepens the complexity.

Narrative Path & Resolution (10/10)

Three seasons, complete. The ending resolves the causality loop, answers the questions, satisfies the philosophy. One of television's most complex narratives, perfectly concluded.
