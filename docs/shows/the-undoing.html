The Undoing

2020 • HBO • 6 Episodes

Score: 7.45
Mystery, Drama

What It Feels Like

Grace Fraser's husband is accused of murder. She thought she knew him. She didn't. Nicole Kidman and Hugh Grant star in David E. Kelley's adaptation of Jean Hanff Korelitz's novel: wealthy Manhattan unraveling, marriage as mystery.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Nicole Kidman as Grace: the wife whose certainty dissolves. Hugh Grant as Jonathan: the charming husband with secrets. Donald Sutherland as the father. The performances are prestige thriller.

World Building (8/10)

Manhattan wealth: the apartments, the schools, the trust funds. The world is upper-class privilege—how it protects and how it fails.

Cinematography (8/10)

HBO production: Manhattan and the Hamptons photographed for wealth and doubt. Susanne Bier directed—the style is psychological thriller.

Visual Spectacle (3/10)

The spectacle is wealth: the coats (so many coats), the apartments, the lifestyle. The "spectacle" is the trial and the doubt.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Marriage as mystery, whether we can know anyone, privilege as protection and blindness, what we sacrifice for appearance. The show explores these through the thriller.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

Did he do it? Each episode reveals more doubts, more secrets. The structure is whodunit—perfect for binge-watching. The pacing is relentless.

Narrative Path & Resolution (7/10)

Complete miniseries. The trial reveals the truth. The ending is satisfying though some found it predictable. Prestige thriller done well.
