Dear Edward

2023 • Apple TV+ • 10 Episodes

Score: 6.85
Drama, Grief

What It Feels Like

A plane crashes. Everyone dies except Edward, a 12-year-old boy. His family was on board. Now he's the sole survivor, and the families of victims want connection to him. Ann Napolitano's novel adapted: grief's many forms, one boy as vessel for collective loss.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (7/10)

Colin O'Brien as Edward: grief embodied. Connie Britton, Taylor Schilling. The ensemble creates the grief community.

World Building (7/10)

Post-crash world: the therapy groups, the families, Edward's new life. The world is collective grief.

Cinematography (7/10)

Apple TV+ production: intimate drama, grief's quiet moments, the visual language of loss.

Visual Spectacle (1/10)

No spectacle. This is grief and recovery. The spectacle is emotional processing.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Grief, survivor's guilt, community in loss, how we carry what's gone. The show explores collective trauma.

Narrative Drive (6/10)

The crash's impact on everyone. Edward's survival. Grief drama structure—deliberate pace.

Narrative Path & Resolution (5/10)

Cancelled after one season. The novel continues; the show doesn't. Incomplete story.
