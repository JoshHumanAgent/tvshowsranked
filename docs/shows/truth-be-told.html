Truth Be Told

2019 - 2023 • Apple TV+ • 29 Episodes

Score: 7.15
Crime, Mystery

What It Feels Like

Poppy Parnell is a true-crime podcaster. Her investigations reopen old cases, question old verdicts, and confront old wounds. Octavia Spencer is Poppy: the journalist whose work both heals and harms. Each season is a new case, a new investigation, a new set of consequences.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Octavia Spencer carries the show: the podcaster with conscience, the investigator with personal stakes. The guest casts (Aaron Paul in season 1, Kate Hudson in season 2) provide the cases. Spencer is the constant.

World Building (7/10)

The podcast world: recording studios, investigation scenes, the families affected by the crimes. The world expands each season—new cases, new victims, new suspects. The setting is contemporary media landscape.

Cinematography (6/10)

Apple TV+ production: true-crime aesthetics rendered for drama. The style is functional—the investigation, the interviews, the reveals. The podcast aesthetic is integrated.

Visual Spectacle (2/10)

No spectacle. This is investigation: interviews, research, confrontations. The "spectacle" is the revelation—watching Poppy uncover the truth. Character-driven mystery.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

True crime as entertainment, whether reopening wounds helps or harms, the ethics of investigation, the cost of truth. The show explores the true-crime phenomenon from within.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Each season investigates a case: what really happened, who's guilty, what's the cost of knowing. The structure is mystery—the drive is the question and the answer.

Narrative Path & Resolution (6/10)

Three seasons. Each season resolves its case; Poppy's character continues. The show explores true-crime ethics across cases. Seasonal mysteries, serialized character.
