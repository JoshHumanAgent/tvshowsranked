What It Feels Like to Watch

A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms adapts George R.R. Martin's Tales of Dunk and Egg— novellas set 90 years before Game of Thrones. It's smaller in scale, intimate in focus, and arguably better for it. Ser Duncan the Tall (Dunk) and his squire Egg wander Westeros as hedge knights, getting entangled in plots they barely understand.

Where Game of Thrones was about thrones and armies, this is about honor and friendship. Dunk is no noble—he's tall, awkward, and perpetually broke—but he has the kind of integrity that legends are made of. Egg is actually Aegon Targaryen, future king, hiding his identity to learn what commoners know.

The production matches House of the Dragon's quality: castle ruins, jousting tournaments, the full texture of Westeros. But the stakes are personal. The Ashford Tourney in the first episodes captures the danger and glory of knight-errantry in ways GoT never attempted.

Visual Spectacle (9): The tournament scenes rival anything in GoT or HotD. The castles feel ancient, lived-in, real. HBO didn't skimp on the budget.
