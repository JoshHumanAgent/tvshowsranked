Superstore

2015 - 2021 • NBC • 113 Episodes

Score: 7.20
Comedy, Workplace

What It Feels Like

Cloud 9 is a big-box store in St. Louis. The employees are working class and underpaid. America Ferrera is Amy: the floor supervisor with dreams. Justin Spitzer created retail as found family—this is what work looks like for millions.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

America Ferrera as Amy. Ben Feldman as Jonah: the rich kid working retail. The ensemble captures the diversity of retail workers—immigrants, students, lifers.

World Building (8/10)

Cloud 9: the store, the break room, the warehouse, the customers. The world is retail reality—understaffed, underpaid, essential.

Cinematography (5/10)

Single-camera sitcom: bright store lighting, the visual language of workplace comedy. Functional.

Visual Spectacle (1/10)

No spectacle. This is a store. The "spectacle" is watching people work retail jobs with dignity.

Conceptual Density (6/10)

Working class America, labor unions, retail as career, immigration. The show explores these through comedy—sometimes sharply.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Amy's career; the will-they-won't-they; labor organizing. The structure is sitcom episodic with serialized threads.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Six seasons, complete. The union storyline resolves; relationships resolve. A satisfying workplace comedy ending.
