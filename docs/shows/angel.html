What It Feels Like to Watch

Angel (David Boreanaz) leaves Buffy behind and moves to Los Angeles. He opens a detective agency. He fights demons. He tries to atone for centuries of evil. That's season one. By season five, he's running a law firm that represents the forces of evil—and trying to change it from the inside.

What starts as a Buffy spinoff becomes something darker and more adult. The tone shifts from noir detective to corporate espionage to apocalyptic fantasy. The ensemble expands: Cordelia (Charisma Carpenter), Wesley (Alexis Denisof), Gunn (J. August Richards), Fred (Amy Acker), Lorne (Andy Hallet). Each character has a full arc, each death feels earned.

The show's central question—can a vampire with a soul find redemption?—is never answered simply. Angel fights for redemption, but his methods become increasingly compromised. The finale is one of television's boldest endings: our heroes charge into battle against impossible odds. Cut to black.
