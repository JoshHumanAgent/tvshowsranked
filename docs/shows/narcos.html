Narcos

2015 - 2017 • Netflix • 30 Episodes

Score: 7.50
Crime, Drama

What It Feels Like

Pablo Escobar rises from smuggler to world's most powerful drug lord. Two DEA agents try to stop him. Wagner Moura is Escobar; Pedro Pascal and Boyd Holbrook are the agents. Colombia in the 1980s: drug money, politics, violence, and Pablo.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Wagner Moura as Pablo: terrifying, human, magnetic. The accent, the presence, the transformation. Pedro Pascal as Peña: the agent who adapts to the war. The performances make the history visceral.

World Building (9/10)

Colombia 1980s: Medellín, the trafficking routes, the political corruption, the violence. The world is specific—history as thriller. The production captures the era.

Cinematography (8/10)

Netflix production: Colombia rendered with period detail, the violence sudden, the scope epic. The style serves the escalation—Pablo's empire building.

Visual Spectacle (7/10)

The spectacle is Pablo's power: the haciendas, the operations, the violence. The production stages the history with scale. Narco-epic.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Power and legitimacy, the American war on drugs, political violence, what violence begets. The show explores these while prioritizing the thriller.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

Pablo rises, Pablo rules, Pablo falls. The structure is rise-and-fall biopic spread across three seasons. The pacing is relentless.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Three seasons, complete. Pablo's story ends; the war continues. The show pivots to Cali cartel in season 3. The Escobar arc is complete.
