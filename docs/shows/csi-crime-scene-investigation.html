CSI: Crime Scene Investigation

2000 - 2015 • CBS • 337 Episodes

Score: 7.15
Procedural, Crime

What It Feels Like

Las Vegas crime scene investigators solve murders through forensic evidence. William Petersen is Gil Grissom: the entomologist leading the night shift. The show that made forensics cool: microscopic evidence, DNA breakthroughs, the science of catching killers. CSI spawned a franchise and influenced real forensic science.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (7/10)

William Petersen as Grissom: the cerebral leader. Marg Helgenberger as Catherine Willows: the former showgirl. The ensemble (George Eads, Jorja Fox, Gary Dourdan, Paul Guilfoyle) creates the team. The performances are procedural professional.

World Building (7/10)

Las Vegas forensic lab: the evidence, the microscopes, the techniques. The world is forensic science as hero—evidence doesn't lie. Las Vegas provides the crime backdrop.

Cinematography (6/10)

Network procedural: bright, clinical, the evidence rendered with visual effects. The style is science-as-spectacle—microscopes, DNA sequences, bullet trajectories. Dated but distinctive.

Visual Spectacle (4/10)

The spectacle is forensic: evidence magnified, crime reconstructions, scientific revelations. The "spectacle" is the science—watching investigators find what others missed. Procedural spectacle.

Conceptual Density (5/10)

Science vs. assumption, evidence doesn't lie, the importance of procedure. The show influenced real forensic science education. The themes are procedural; the impact was cultural.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Case of the week with limited serialization: each episode is a self-contained investigation. The structure is reliable—crime, evidence, solution. 337 episodes of consistency.

Narrative Path & Resolution (7/10)

15 seasons with conclusion. Grissom left; Ted Danson led later seasons. The finale brought back original cast for proper goodbye. Complete within its format.
