What It Feels Like

Nikita escaped Division, the secret agency that trained her as assassin. Now she's taking them down. Maggie Q is Nikita: action star with depth. The CW remade the French film as spy thriller series.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (7/10)

Maggie Q as Nikita: action star with emotional depth. Shane West as Michael: her handler turned ally. The CW ensemble approach to spy drama.

World Building (7/10)

Division: the secret assassin training program, the recruits, the missions. The world is spy thriller rendered for television.

Cinematography (6/10)

CW production: action sequences on TV budget, the visual language of spy thriller. Functional action.

Visual Spectacle (6/10)

Action sequences, spy missions, the spectacle of TV action. Better fight choreography than most network shows.

Conceptual Density (6/10)

Government overreach, redemption, whether we can escape our pasts. The show explores these through action-spy format.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Take down Division; protect the new recruits. The structure is mission-of-the-week with serialized conspiracy.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Four seasons, complete. Nikita's story resolves with satisfaction. The CW spy thriller that earned its ending.
