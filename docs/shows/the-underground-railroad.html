What It Feels Like to Watch

Barry Jenkins adapts Colson Whitehead's novel—the Underground Railroad made literal, a subway system beneath the antebellum South. Cora (Thuso Mbedu) escapes slavery, traveling through states that represent different American nightmares. Each episode is a chapter, each state a different horror.

Jenkins' camera creates museum-quality images. The violence is explicit but never gratuitous. The perspective is unflinching—this is American slavery through the eyes of the enslaved.
