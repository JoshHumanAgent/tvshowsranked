Arcane

2021 - 2024 • Netflix • 18 Episodes

Score: 8.15
Animation, Fantasy

What It Feels Like

Piltover is the city of progress. Zaun is the undercity beneath it. Two sisters are divided by the cities' war. League of Legends adapted by Riot Games and Fortiche: steampunk fantasy, family tragedy, the cost of progress. Animation as prestige drama.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Vi and Jinx: sisters divided by tragedy. Voiced by Hailee Steinfeld and Ella Purnell. The characters develop across tragedy and trauma. The voice acting matches the animation's intensity.

World Building (10/10)

Piltover and Zaun: the city above, the city below, hextech, shimmer. The world is complete—steampunk fantasy with specific rules. Every detail matters.

Cinematography (10/10)

Fortiche animation: painted style, steampunk design, action sequences that rival live-action. This is feature-quality animation. The aesthetic is unique.

Visual Spectacle (10/10)

The spectacle is constant: hextech, shimmer, action sequences, the cities. Animation allows anything—Arcane delivers everything. Visually stunning.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Class warfare, family vs. ideology, the cost of progress, addiction, trauma. The show explores adult themes through animation. Not for children.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

The sisters separated, the cities at war, hextech as weapon. The structure is tragedy—watching the divide widen. The drive is relentless.

Narrative Path & Resolution (9/10)

Season 1 complete; season 2 concludes. The story of Vi and Jinx is told. The ending provides resolution while leaving room for continuation. Masterful animation.
