2022 - Present • Netflix • Ongoing

Score: 6.85

What It Feels Like

A high school becomes ground zero for a zombie outbreak. Students trapped inside must survive. Korean zombie horror with social commentary: bullies, class, the failures of adults.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (7/10)

Teen ensemble: survivors, bullies, the infected. The performances capture high school dynamics under apocalypse.

World Building (8/10)

Hyosan High School: the classrooms, the roof, the science lab that started it all. The world is familiar made deadly.

Cinematography (8/10)

Netflix production: fast zombies, claustrophobic hallways, the scale of outbreak. Kinetic zombie action.

Visual Spectacle (8/10)

Zombie hordes, school escapes, military response. Korean zombie production at scale.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Bullying, adult failure, class divide, survival morality. The show explores Korean social issues through zombie genre.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

Survive the school; escape the city. High-stakes survival horror with relentless pace.

Narrative Path & Resolution (6/10)

Season 1 complete; season 2 announced. Some survivors; the outbreak continues.
