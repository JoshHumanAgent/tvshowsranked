Ted Lasso

2020 - 2023 • Apple TV+ • 34 Episodes

Score: 7.65

What It Feels Like

An American football coach is hired to manage a British football club. He knows nothing about soccer. Ted Lasso is kind in a cruel world. Jason Sudeikis created the anti-cynicism football comedy: winning matters less than being good people.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Jason Sudeikis as Ted: unrelenting kindness. Hannah Waddingham as Rebecca: the boss with a heart. Brett Goldstein as Roy Kent. Phil Dunster as Jamie Tartt. Juno Temple as Keeley. Ensemble perfection.

World Building (8/10)

AFC Richmond: the locker room, the pitch, the pub, the British football culture. The world is warmth in a cynical sport.

Cinematography (7/10)

Apple TV+ production: Richmond matches, London locations, the warmth of the show's aesthetic.

Visual Spectacle (3/10)

Football matches, the pitch. The spectacle is emotional—goals that matter because characters care.

Conceptual Density (9/10)

Kindness as strength, mental health, found family, redemption, winning vs. character. The show explores these with genuine warmth.

Narrative Drive (8/10)

Season 1: save Richmond from Revenge. Season 2: Ted's panic attacks. Season 3: promotion. The drive is character growth.

Narrative Path & Resolution (9/10)

Three seasons, complete. Perfect ending—everyone gets what they need. Ted's arc concludes with grace.
