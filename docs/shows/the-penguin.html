What It Feels Like to Watch

Colin Farrell's Oz Cobb—the Penguin—gets his own series, and it's Scarface via Gotham. No Batman, no CGI spectacle, just one man climbing the criminal hierarchy through murder, manipulation, and maternal trauma.

Farrell is unrecognizable under prosthetics, playing Penguin as desperate, clever, and deeply damaged. Cristin Milioti's Sofia Falcone is the rival—recently released from Arkham, equally damaged, equally dangerous. Their war for Gotham's underworld is the show's engine.

Eight episodes of crime saga with comic-book roots. The finale delivers the rise—Oz wins, but at a cost that reveals his damage. The show works entirely without prior Batman knowledge.
