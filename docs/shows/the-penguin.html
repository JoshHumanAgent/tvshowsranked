What It Feels Like to Watch

The Penguin premiered on HBO on September 19, 2024, created by Lauren LeFranc as a spin-off from Matt Reeves' 2022 film The Batman. The series follows Oswald Cobb (Colin Farrell), known as "Oz" or "the Penguin," a disfigured low-level enforcer for the Falcone crime family who sees opportunity in the power vacuum following the death of crime boss Carmine Falcone. Set in the weeks after The Batman, the series depicts Oz's ruthless climb through Gotham's criminal underworld toward becoming the city's new kingpin.

The series distinguishes itself from typical comic book adaptations through its grounded, noir tone—this is The Batman's Gotham, grounded in urban decay and organized crime rather than supervillain spectacle. Oz is not a cartoon penguin but a disabled working-class criminal whose deformity and short stature make him an underdog even among criminals. The series uses his physical difference to explore power, humiliation, and ambition—Oz has been underestimated his entire life and is determined to prove everyone wrong.

Oz's relationship with Victor Aguilar (Rhenzy Feliz), a teenage street kid who becomes his driver and protégé, provides the series' emotional center. Oz sees himself in Victor—another underestimated kid from the wrong side of Gotham—and their surrogate father-son dynamic is genuinely touching even as Oz teaches Victor criminal craft. Oz's mother Francis (a scene-stealing Deirdre O'Connell), suffering from dementia, anchors his humanity; his love for her is genuine even as everything else is calculated.

The power players: Sofia Falcone (Cristin Milioti), Carmine's daughter, returning from Arkham Asylum and seeking revenge for her brother's death and her father's betrayal; Alberto Falcone (Michael Zegen), the weak son who expects to inherit; various crime family lieutenants and city officials who must choose sides. The series operates like a crime thriller—alliances shift, betrayals mount, bodies fall.

Colin Farrell's transformation is remarkable—under prosthetics that render him unrecognizable, Farrell creates a fully realized character through voice and physicality. His Oz is working-class, clever, dangerous, funny in a grim way, and always calculating. It's a performance that would be impressive without the makeup; with it, it's transformative work that earned Farrell the Golden Globe.

The eight-episode limited series structure allows tight narrative focus—Oz's rise is plotted with precision, each episode advancing his criminal enterprise while deepening character relationships. The finale, with Oz triumphant but isolated, suggests the cost of power. HBO has announced a second season, indicating the Penguin's story continues.
