The Haunting of Bly Manor

2020 • Netflix • 9 Episodes

Score: 7.45
Horror, Romance

What It Feels Like

An au pair goes to Bly Manor to care for two orphans. There are ghosts. But this is also a love story. Mike Flanagan adapts Henry James: romantic horror, the Turn of the Screw expanded, love and loss in a haunted house.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Victoria Pedretti as Dani: the au pair with secrets. The supporting cast creates the Bly household. The ghost of the lady in the lake gives the horror. The performances capture both romance and dread.

World Building (9/10)

Bly Manor: the house, the lake, the ghosts, the history. The world is layered—past and present ghosts coexist. The manor is wound and healing.

Cinematography (8/10)

Netflix production: English manor photographed for gothic romance. The style is warmer than Hill House—this is love story as much as horror.

Visual Spectacle (7/10)

The spectacle is the ghosts: the lady in the lake, the faceless children, the hidden presences. The horror is patient—revealed through story.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Love and loss, what we hold onto after death, whether ghosts are the dead or our memories, the cost of holding on. The show explores romance through haunting.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Dani arrives, falls in love, discovers the ghosts. The structure is slower than Hill House—romance requires patience. The drive is revelation.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Complete miniseries. The love story resolves; the haunting resolves. The ending is romantic tragedy. Beautiful but gentler than Hill House.
