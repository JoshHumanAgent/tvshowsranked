# Shōgun (2024)

**Rank #53** | Drama, Historical, Action | 2024 | FX | 1+ Season

---

## What It Feels Like to Watch

In 1600 Japan, a century-defining civil war is brewing. Lord Yoshii Toranaga, a powerful daimyo, finds himself betrayed by his rivals on the Council of Regents. Meanwhile, a mysterious European ship washes up in a nearby fishing village, carrying an English pilot named John Blackthorne who will change everything.

This is the new adaptation of James Clavell's novel, and it is magnificent.

The 2024 version surpasses the 1980 miniseries in almost every respect. Where the original was sprawling and somewhat Westernized, this version immerses you fully in feudal Japan. Large portions are in Japanese with subtitles. The politics are complex, the social codes are intricate, and the show trusts you to keep up.

Hiroyuki Sanada anchors the series as Toranaga — a man playing a long game while surrounded by enemies. He communicates volumes with a glance, a silence, a barely perceptible shift in posture. This is one of the great television performances.

Cosmo Jarvis plays Blackthorne, the "barbarian" who must learn to navigate a culture he doesn't understand. Anna Sawai is Lady Mariko, a Christian convert who serves as translator and becomes the series' most fascinating character — caught between worlds, between loyalties, between who she was and who she's become.

The production design is stunning. The costumes, the castles, the landscapes — everything feels authentic and lived-in. The action sequences are brutal and tactical, emphasizing strategy over spectacle.

What makes *Shōgun* essential is its patience. This is slow television that rewards attention. Every conversation is chess. Every alliance is temporary. Every decision ripples outward.

The series asks what it means to be loyal, what it means to be civilized, and whether the two are compatible.
