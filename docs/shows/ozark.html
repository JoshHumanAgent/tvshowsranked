What It Feels Like to Watch

Breaking Bad as family drama with better financial planning. Jason Bateman's Marty Byrde is a financial advisor laundering money for a Mexican cartel, forced to move his family to the Missouri Ozarks when a scheme goes wrong. Laura Linney's Wendy is the wife who discovers she's better at this than he is.

The show's thesis: everyone is capable of anything when threatened. The Byrdes transform from suburban professionals to cartel operatives, rationalizing each escalation. The body count rises. The schemes complicate. The cartel, the local criminals, the FBI—all converge on the lake.

Julia Garner's Ruth Langmore is the standout—a local criminal who becomes Marty's lieutenant, delivering lines like "You're gonna make me rich" with feral intensity. The supporting cast (Lisa Emery, Tom Pelphrey, Janet McTeer) provides villains and complications.

Four seasons of escalating stakes, ending with the show's thesis made explicit: the Byrdes escape, but at a cost that reveals who they've become.
