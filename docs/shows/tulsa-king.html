Tulsa King

2022 - Present • Paramount+ • Ongoing

Score: 7.15
Crime, Drama

What It Feels Like

Dwight Manfredi finishes 25 years in prison. The Mafia gives him Tulsa, Oklahoma as territory. He builds a crew from misfits. Sylvester Stallone is Dwight: old-school mobster in new-world America. Taylor Sheridan creates a fish-out-of-water crime dramedy: Goodfellas meets Tulsa.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Sylvester Stallone as Dwight: the aging gangster, out of time but sharp. The supporting cast creates his Tulsa crew—locals who become his soldiers. Stallone embraces the comedy and the threat.

World Building (7/10)

Tulsa, Oklahoma: not traditional Mafia territory. The world is Dwight's operation expanding into unfamiliar terrain. The contrast between New York mob and Oklahoma is the premise.

Cinematography (7/10)

Paramount+ production: Tulsa rendered with Western aesthetics, the criminal operations staged with Sheridan-style. The style is modern Western crime.

Visual Spectacle (5/10)

The spectacle is criminal operation: shakedowns, negotiations, turf establishment. Less violent than traditional mob shows; more comedy. The spectacle is fish-out-of-water.

Conceptual Density (6/10)

Old world vs. new world, whether the old codes still apply, starting over late in life, loyalty in modern America. The show explores these through Dwight's Tulsa adventure.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Dwight builds his operation, navigates Tulsa, deals with the New York mob's expectations. The structure is empire-building with criminal comedy. The drive is establishment.

Narrative Path & Resolution (5/10)

Ongoing. Two seasons completed. The operation continues to build. No announced endpoint—Taylor Sheridan shows tend to run until they stop.
