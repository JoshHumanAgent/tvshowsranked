Ghosts (UK)

2019 - 2023 • BBC • 37 Episodes

Score: 7.35
Comedy, Fantasy

What It Feels Like

Alison inherits a crumbling manor house. She falls out a window, nearly dies, and now can see ghosts. The house is full of them—centuries of previous inhabitants. The creators of Horrible Histories made the warmest comedy about death.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Charlotte Ritchie as Alison: the living person adjusting to ghosts. Kiell Smith-Bynoe as Mike: the husband who can't see them. The ghost ensemble (Mathew Baynton, Simon Farnaby, Martha Howe-Douglas, etc.) creates centuries of characters.

World Building (8/10)

Button House: the manor, the ghosts from different eras, the rules of haunting. The world is built through character—each ghost is their century, their death, their stuckness.

Cinematography (6/10)

BBC comedy production: the manor, the ghosts rendered with simple effects. The style is functional—this is character comedy, not visual spectacle.

Visual Spectacle (2/10)

No spectacle. This is people (and ghosts) talking in a house. The "spectacle" is the character work—watching centuries interact.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Death and what comes after, being stuck, finding meaning in eternity, history as human comedy. The show explores these with warmth and humor.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Alison adjusts to ghosts; the ghosts adjust to her; the house must be saved. The structure is episodic comedy—each episode explores character.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Five seasons, complete. The finale provides closure—ghosts and living find peace. One of the kindest comedies ever made.
