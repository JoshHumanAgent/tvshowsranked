Peep Show

2003 - 2015 • Channel 4 • 54 Episodes

Score: 7.55
Comedy

What It Feels Like

Mark and Jeremy are flatmates in Croydon. Mark is a loan officer with anxiety. Jeremy is a wannabe musician with delusions. We hear their thoughts. Jesse Armstrong and Sam Bain created the longest-running Channel 4 comedy—failure as art.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

David Mitchell as Mark: anxiety made flesh. Robert Webb as Jeremy: delusion incarnate. Their internal monologues reveal everything they won't say. Iconic British comedy duo.

World Building (7/10)

Croydon flat: the office, the parties, the failed relationships. The world is disappointment—the specific texture of British male failure.

Cinematography (6/10)

Point-of-view camera: we see what they see, hear what they think. The style is intimate—sometimes uncomfortably close.

Visual Spectacle (1/10)

No spectacle. This is failure in a flat. The "spectacle" is watching people make terrible choices.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Male friendship, delusion vs. anxiety, the lies we tell ourselves, British class dynamics. The show explores failure with uncomfortable honesty.

Narrative Drive (6/10)

Mark and Jeremy fail at work, relationships, life. Episodic disasters. The drive is schadenfreude.

Narrative Path & Resolution (7/10)

Nine seasons, complete. Mark and Jeremy remain themselves. The show never betrays its premise. Consistency as achievement.
