Slow Horses

2022 - Present • Apple TV+ • Ongoing

Score: 7.75
Spy, Thriller

What It Feels Like

Slough House is where MI5 sends the screw-ups. Jackson Lamb is their drunk, brilliant, abusive boss. Gary Oldman is Lamb: the spy who doesn't care. Mick Herron's novels adapted: spy thriller as dark comedy, the rejects who keep saving the day.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (10/10)

Gary Oldman as Jackson Lamb: the grossness, the brilliance, the hidden decency. Jack Lowden as River Cartwright: the talented one who blew it. The ensemble creates the rejects. Oldman's Lamb is iconic.

World Building (8/10)

Slough House: the dingy office, the rejected spies, the assignments nobody wants. The world is the opposite of MI5 glamour—this is where careers go to die.

Cinematography (7/10)

Apple TV+ production: London photographed for spy thriller, Slough House rendered as dump. The style serves the premise—the rejects operate in the shadows.

Visual Spectacle (5/10)

The spectacle is spy work: surveillance, action, the occasional gunfight. The "spectacle" is Lamb being terrible. Spy thriller with dark comedy.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Failure vs. competence, bureaucracy vs. effectiveness, what we owe to colleagues, the secrets services keep. The show explores these through the rejects' work.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

Each season is a mission: the slow horses prove themselves, Lamb saves them. The structure is spy thriller—the drive is the assignment.

Narrative Path & Resolution (5/10)

Ongoing. Three seasons complete, more planned. Each season resolves its mission; the characters continue. No endpoint.
