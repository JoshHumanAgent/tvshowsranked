High Maintenance

2016 - 2020 • HBO • 19 Episodes

Score: 7.18

What It Feels Like

The Guy delivers weed to New Yorkers. Each episode is about his clients—their lives, their problems, their loneliness. Ben Sinclair created and stars in the web-series-turned-HBO show. Not about cannabis, but about connection in the city. New York as mosaic.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Ben Sinclair as The Guy: the witness, the connection, the through-line. The guest stars create a rotating cast of New Yorkers. The performances are naturalistic—this feels like real people.

World Building (9/10)

Brooklyn, Manhattan: the apartments, the neighborhoods, the subcultures. Each episode is a different world connected by The Guy. New York rendered with specificity and love.

Cinematography (7/10)

HBO production with indie aesthetics: handheld, observational, the city photographed in its variety. The style is invisible—we're watching lives, not a show.

Visual Spectacle (2/10)

No spectacle. This is lives lived: apartments, conversations, deliveries. The "spectacle" is the variety of human experience. Intimate portraiture.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Loneliness, connection, how we survive the city, the transactions that aren't transactions, New York as character. The show explores urban existence through the weed delivery structure. Profoundly human.

Narrative Drive (6/10)

Episodic anthology: each episode is a different client's story. The Guy provides continuity but not drive. This is observation, not plot. The drive is curiosity about lives.

Narrative Path & Resolution (6/10)

Four seasons. The show ended without dramatic conclusion—it's a slice of life that stopped being sliced. The format resists traditional resolution. Complete in its own terms.
