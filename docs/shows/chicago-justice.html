Chicago Justice

2017 • NBC • 13 Episodes

Score: 6.78
Legal, Drama, Crime

What It Feels Like

The State's Attorney's office in Chicago prosecutes crimes, navigates politics, and argues in courtroom—all under the Dick Wolf brand. Chicago Justice is Law & Order: Chicago, with lawyers instead of cops. Philip Winchester plays ASA Peter Stone, a former baseball player turned prosecutor; Joelle Carter is his investigator; Carl Weathers is their boss. The show is procedural comfort: case of the week, legal arguments, personal drama, verdict. It's competent Wolf production—nothing innovative but nothing offensive. Cancelled after one season, it remains a footnote in the Chicago franchise. Not the best legal drama; not the worst.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (6/10)

Philip Winchester is solid as lead prosecutor—he carries the show's gravitas. Carl Weathers is reliable as the DA. The supporting cast prosecutes and investigates competently. No character is memorable; everyone is functional.

World Building (5/10)

Chicago legal system: State's Attorney's office, courtrooms, police precincts. The Chicago franchise shares characters; crossovers happen. It's network procedural world-building—reliable, not distinctive.

Cinematography (5/10)

Standard procedural footage. Courtrooms, offices, interrogation rooms. Dick Wolf production values—clean, professional, forgettable.

Visual Spectacle (2/10)

No spectacle. Legal drama. The "action" is courtroom argument.

Conceptual Density (4/10)

Justice, prosecution ethics, politics in the DA's office. The themes are procedural standard-fare. The show is about winning cases, not exploring systems.

Narrative Drive (5/10)

Case-of-week structure with personal serialization. The legal beats are familiar—investigation, discovery, courtroom. Competent without momentum.

Narrative Path & Resolution (4/10)

One season, 13 episodes. Cancelled without resolution. The franchise continues with other Chicago shows; Justice ended as the weakest link. Fine procedural, forgettable drama.
