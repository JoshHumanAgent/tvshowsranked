What It Feels Like to Watch

The experience is singular. Where most crime dramas seduce you with the promise of catharsis‚Äîbad guys caught, cases closed, order restored‚ÄîThe Wire operates with tragic inevitability. It is not pleasurable in any conventional sense. It is necessary. It is devastating. It is, quite simply, the greatest achievement in the history of the medium.

The Wire operates with the narrative engine of a great novel‚Äîslow to start, complex in structure, patient in payoff, devastating in conclusion. The show's serialized structure‚Äîeach season functions as a standalone novel while contributing to an overarching narrative‚Äîwas revolutionary for its time.
