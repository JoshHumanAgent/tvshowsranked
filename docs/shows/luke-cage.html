Luke Cage

2016 - 2018 • Netflix • 26 Episodes

Score: 7.10
Superhero, Action

What It Feels Like

Luke Cage is bulletproof. He's hiding in Harlem. But Harlem needs him. Mike Colter is Luke: strong, reluctant, heroic. The Netflix Marvel show that was unapologetically Black: Harlem's defender, community as character, hip-hop as soundtrack.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Mike Colter as Luke: presence and strength. Mahershala Ali as Cottonmouth: villain with depth. Alfre Woodard, Simone Missick. The cast created Harlem's world.

World Building (8/10)

Harlem: the barbershop, the nightclub, the streets. The world is Black community rendered with specificity.

Cinematography (7/10)

Netflix Marvel: Harlem texture, hip-hop aesthetic, the visual language of street hero.

Visual Spectacle (6/10)

Super strength, bulletproof action. The spectacle is street-level heroism.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Black community, gentrification, heroism as responsibility. The show explored race and justice through superhero.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Harlem needs saving; Luke steps up. Each season: villain, community, hero choice.

Narrative Path & Resolution (5/10)

Two seasons, cancelled with Defenders. The story continues in other shows but incomplete.
