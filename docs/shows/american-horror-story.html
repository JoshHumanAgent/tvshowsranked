American Horror Story

2011 - Present • FX • 130+ Episodes

Score: 7.10
Horror, Anthology

What It Feels Like

Each season is a different horror story: Murder House, Asylum, Coven, Freak Show, Hotel, Roanoke, Cult, Apocalypse, 1984, Double Feature, NYC, Delicate. Ryan Murphy creates horror anthology with the same actors playing different roles. Campy, scary, inconsistent.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (8/10)

Jessica Lange. Sarah Paulson. Evan Peters. Frances Conroy. Kathy Bates. The ensemble returns each season in new roles. Some performances are iconic.

World Building (7/10)

Each season builds a horror world. Some seasons connect (Coven/Apocalypse). The world is whatever Murphy wants it to be.

Cinematography (7/10)

FX production: stylish horror, different aesthetics each season. The show is visually ambitious.

Visual Spectacle (7/10)

Depends on the season. Asylum and Hotel are visually rich; Roanoke is found footage. The spectacle is horror imagery.

Conceptual Density (6/10)

Varies wildly by season. Some explore interesting themes; others are just scary stuff happening.

Narrative Drive (7/10)

Each season has its own narrative. The drive is surviving the horror.

Narrative Path & Resolution (5/10)

Ongoing anthology. Some seasons end well; others don't. Inconsistent quality.
