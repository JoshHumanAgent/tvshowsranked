Mr. Show

1995 - 1998 • HBO • 30 Episodes

Score: 7.55
Sketch, Comedy

What It Feels Like

Bob Odenkirk and David Cross created the most influential sketch show you've never seen. HBO's first comedy series: absurdist, interconnected sketches, the template for every alt-comedy show that followed.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Bob Odenkirk and David Cross: the comedy duo. Future stars (Sarah Silverman, Paul F. Tompkins). The performances pioneered alternative comedy.

World Building (6/10)

Sketch world: bits connect, themes emerge. The world is the show's absurdist logic.

Cinematography (4/10)

90s HBO: no budget but brilliant writing. The style is sketch comedy basics.

Visual Spectacle (1/10)

No spectacle. This is sketch comedy in a warehouse. The spectacle is the writing.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Satire, absurdity, connecting sketches into larger meanings. The show is comedy as art.

Narrative Drive (6/10)

Sketch shows don't have narrative. The drive is surprise and connection.

Narrative Path & Resolution (7/10)

Four seasons, complete. The show ended before decline. Influential and complete.
