Behind Her Eyes

2021 • Netflix • 6 Episodes

Score: 7.20
Psychological Thriller

What It Feels Like

Louise is a single mother who begins an affair with her boss, David. She also befriends his wife, Adele. Neither knows about the other. As Louise learns more about Adele and David's past, the story twists into unexpected territory. Sarah Pinborough's novel adapted: a thriller that starts as one thing and becomes another. The ending is notorious.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (7/10)

Simi Brown as Louise is relatable and ultimately devastating. Tom Bateman as David is the husband with secrets. Eve Hewson as Adele is the wife with more secrets. The performances require shifts in audience sympathy; the actors navigate the turns.

World Building (7/10)

London present and past: the affair, the friendship, the secrets that accumulate. The world expands as the story unfolds—what seems like domestic thriller becomes something else. The world is built for the twist.

Cinematography (6/10)

Netflix thriller aesthetics: London rendered with shadow, intimacy photographed with suggestion. The style serves the twist without revealing it. Functional tension-building.

Visual Spectacle (3/10)

The spectacle is the twist: what seems like psychological thriller becomes something with supernatural implications. The "spectacle" is narrative—audience realization.

Conceptual Density (6/10)

Obsession, manipulation, the stories we tell ourselves, whether we can truly know others. The themes are present but the twist is the priority. Discussion of the ending dominates conversation.

Narrative Drive (9/10)

Six episodes of escalating tension. Each episode reveals something that reframes the story. The drive is relentless—the viewer must know what happens. The twist is built for binge-watching and discussion.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Complete miniseries. The ending is the point—controversial, divisive, unforgettable. The show is designed for the twist; the twist is designed for conversation. Whether it works depends on the viewer's tolerance for the supernatural.
