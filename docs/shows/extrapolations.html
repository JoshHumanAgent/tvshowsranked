Extrapolations

2023 • Apple TV+ • 8 Episodes

Score: 6.65
Anthology, Climate

What It Feels Like

An anthology about climate change over the coming decades. Each episode is a different year, different characters, different impact. Scott Z. Burns created climate fiction as television: earnest but uneven.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (6/10)

Star-studded cast (Meryl Streep, Edward Norton, Marion Cotillard) in anthology stories. Some episodes work better than others.

World Building (8/10)

Future Earth: each decade more devastated. The world-building is the point—showing climate collapse gradually, believably.

Cinematography (7/10)

Apple TV+ production: future rendered with environmental disaster. High production values.

Visual Spectacle (6/10)

Future cities, environmental collapse, the spectacle of disaster approaching. Earnest environmental filmmaking.

Conceptual Density (8/10)

Climate change, our responsibility, the future we're creating. This is message television—sometimes didactic, sometimes moving.

Narrative Drive (5/10)

Anthology structure: each episode standalone. The drive is thematic rather than narrative.

Narrative Path & Resolution (6/10)

Complete anthology. No unified story—only the climate thread connects. Mixed success.
