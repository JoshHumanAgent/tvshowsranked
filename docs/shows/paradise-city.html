What It Feels Like

A young rock band on tour discovers the music industry is darker than they imagined. Paradise City is the spiritual sequel to American Satan: occult rock music, industry exploitation, and genuine supernatural horror. Andy Biersack plays Johnny Faust, a singer navigating fame and temptation. The show has real rock stars—Andy Black, Asking Alexandria, Black Veil Brides—playing fictionalized versions. The occult elements are treated seriously: making deals, blood sacrifices, and cosmic horror. The production values exceed the genre—Amazon gave this indie rock production budget. The supernatural horror serves the music industry satire: selling your soul is literal. For fans of metal, horror, and the dark side of fame.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (6.5/10)

Andy Biersack plays a version of himself—not a trained actor but authentic. The musicians cast bring legitimacy to rock world. The occult supporting cast adds horror experience. The acting is uneven but appropriate to genre.

World Building (7/10)

Touring musicians, occult industry, supernatural contracts. The world feels like the rock scene knows these characters exist. The horror is specific to music world. Fans recognize the references.

Cinematography (7/10)

Music video aesthetic applied to horror. Concerts are bright; occult sequences are dark. The visual style matches the tone—glamorous and threatening.

Visual Spectacle (6/10)

Concerts, occult rituals, music industry excess. The spectacle is rock-star glamour meeting supernatural horror. The budget shows in occult sequences.

Conceptual Density (6/10)

Fame and cost. Selling your soul is literal and metaphor. The music industry eats artists. The occult horror is vehicle for industry critique.

Narrative Drive (6.5/10)

Tour escalates. Temptations multiply. Occult forces close in. The show builds steadily toward Faustian conclusion.

Narrative Path & Resolution (6/10)

Season 1 is complete story. Johnny's choices have consequences. The ending sets up American Satan crossover. Satisfying for fans of the scene.
