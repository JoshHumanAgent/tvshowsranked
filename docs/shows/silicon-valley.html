What It Feels Like to Watch

Richard Hendricks (Thomas Middleditch) creates a compression algorithm. It's brilliant—so brilliant that every tech company in Palo Alto wants it. He and his friends from a hacker hostel try to build a company. And fail. And try again. And fail again. And somehow, against all odds, succeed.

Mike Judge worked in Silicon Valley in the 1980s. His satire is precise: the jargon, the pretension, the money, the delusion. Every character is recognizable from the real tech ecosystem. Gavin Belson (Matt Ross) is every tech CEO who ever claimed to be changing the world. Erlich Bachman (T.J. Miller) is every founder who peaked early.

The show's secret weapon is its technical accuracy. The compression algorithms are real. The business struggles are real. The jokes land because they're based on truth. This is satire with teeth.
