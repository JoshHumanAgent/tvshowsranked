This Country

2017 - 2020 • BBC • 19 Episodes

Score: 7.35
Comedy, Mockumentary

What It Feels Like

Cousins Kerry and Kurtan are 20-somethings in rural Gloucestershire. They're bored in the countryside. Daisy May Cooper and Charlie Cooper (real siblings) created British cringe comedy: small-town life, family gossip, and no prospects.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (9/10)

Daisy May Cooper as Kerry: desperate for drama. Charlie Cooper as Kurtan: ambition without opportunity. The sibling chemistry is real—the awkwardness is authentic.

World Building (8/10)

The Cotswolds: villages, fields, limited prospects. The world is rural England's boredom and beauty.

Cinematography (6/10)

Mockumentary style: handheld, talking heads, the village. Simple but effective.

Visual Spectacle (1/10)

Countryside. That's it. The spectacle is watching people be bored.

Conceptual Density (7/10)

Rural decline, aspiration vs. reality, family dysfunction. The show explores English countryside through comedy.

Narrative Drive (6/10)

Episodic village drama. Kurtan's schemes; Kerry's dramas. The drive is character comedy.

Narrative Path & Resolution (8/10)

Three seasons, complete. The ending is perfect—growth without leaving. Heartfelt conclusion.
