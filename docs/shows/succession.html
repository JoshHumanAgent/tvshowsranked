Succession

2018 - 2023 • HBO • 39 Episodes

Score: 8.25
Drama, Satire

What It Feels Like

Logan Roy runs a media empire. His children want to inherit. They're awful. He's worse. Jesse Armstrong created Shakespearean tragedy as corporate satire: King Lear meets Murdoch, the Roys tearing each other apart for daddy's approval.

The 7-Dimension Analysis

Characters & Acting (10/10)

Brian Cox as Logan: the tyrant, the father, the void. Jeremy Strong as Kendall: the son who can't escape. Sarah Snook as Shiv: the daughter who can't win. Kieran Culkin as Roman: the chaos agent. Matthew Macfadyen as Tom: the survivor. The ensemble is the decade's best.

World Building (9/10)

Media empire capitalism: the boardrooms, the private jets, the Waystar Royco empire, the political connections. The world is wealth as weapon—every resource deployed in the succession fight.

Cinematography (8/10)

HBO production: the private jets, the mansions, the corporate headquarters. The style is intimate chaos—handheld cameras in billionaires' palaces.

Visual Spectacle (4/10)

The spectacle is wealth: the jets, the weddings, the funerals. The "spectacle" is the toxicity—watching rich people destroy each other.

Conceptual Density (9/10)

Inheritance, what we owe to parents, whether love is possible in capitalism, media power, corruption as system. The show explores these through Shakespearean family tragedy.

Narrative Drive (10/10)

Who will inherit? Each season escalates the fight. The structure is succession—corporate and familial. The dialogue is sharper than anything on television.

Narrative Path & Resolution (10/10)

Four seasons, complete. The finale is among television's most debated. The succession resolved; the tragedy complete. Essential television.
