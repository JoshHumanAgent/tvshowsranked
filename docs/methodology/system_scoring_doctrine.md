# TV Shows Ranked: System Scoring Doctrine

## Overview

This methodology documents the ranking system used to evaluate and rank the top 100 television shows. Our approach combines quantitative metrics from major review aggregators with qualitative assessments across five core dimensions.

## Ranking Dimensions

### 1. Narrative Ambition (10 points)
Measures the scope, complexity, and originality of the storytelling.

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **10**: Epic multi-season arcs with groundbreaking structure; novel approaches to storytelling
- **8-9**: Ambitious plotting across multiple seasons; innovative narrative techniques
- **6-7**: Solid storytelling with consistent quality; occasional ambition
- **Below 6**: Formulaic or conventional approaches; limited evolution

### 2. Character Depth (10 points)
Evaluates the complexity, development, and psychological realism of characters.

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **10**: Iconic characters with multiple dimensions; memorable ensemble casts
- **8-9**: Strong character development; complex protagonist/antagonist relationships
- **6-7**: Competent character work; some notable performances
- **Below 6**: Flat or stereotypical characters; limited development

### 3. Thematic Resonance (10 points)
Assesses the depth of themes, philosophical questions, and cultural relevance.

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **10**: Profound exploration of universal themes; culturally transformative
- **8-9**: Thought-provoking themes with lasting relevance
- **6-7**: Meaningful themes explored adequately
- **Below 6**: Surface-level or absent thematic content

### 4. Cultural Impact (10 points)
Measures influence on television, broader culture, and industry practices.

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **10**: Changed the television landscape; created new genres or formats
- **8-9**: Significant influence; widely referenced or imitated
- **6-7**: Notable cultural presence during original run
- **Below 6**: Limited cultural footprint

### 5. Watchability (10 points)
Evaluates entertainment value, pacing, and engagement.

**Scoring Criteria:**
- **10**: Unmissable; impossible to stop watching
- **8-9**: Highly engaging; rewarding binges
- **6-7**: Entertaining with occasional dips in pacing
- **Below 6**: Difficult to maintain engagement

## Data Sources

### Primary Aggregators
- **IMDb**: User ratings (1-10 scale)
- **Rotten Tomatoes**: Tomatometer scores (0-100%)
- **Metacritic**: Critic scores (0-100)

### Additional Lists
- Wikipedia "Greatest TV Shows" rankings
- Industry publications (Variety, Rolling Stone)
- Critical consensus from major publications

### Date Accessed
All external ratings accessed on: 2026-02-15

## Final Score Calculation

The final score is calculated as a weighted average:

```
Final Score = (Narrative × 0.20) + (Character × 0.25) + (Thematic × 0.20) + (Impact × 0.15) + (Watchability × 0.20)
```

## Non-Spoiler Policy

All consensus highlights and descriptions follow strict non-spoiler guidelines:
- No plot events, twists, or endings revealed
- Focus on "what it feels like to watch" rather than "what happens"
- Character descriptions emphasize journey, not destination
- Themes explored without spoiling specific implementations

## Inclusion Criteria

Shows must meet the following criteria:
1. Minimum of one completed season
2. Available for streaming or home media
3. English-language or significant English audience
4. Critical recognition (minimum 70% RT or 7.0 IMDb)
