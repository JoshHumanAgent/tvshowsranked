## üé≠ CHARACTERS - THE FOUR TESTS

A high-scoring character must have:
1. **Agency** - Choices feel authentically theirs, not plot-driven
2. **Interiority** - Rich inner life, feel real when camera's off
3. **Productive Mystery** - You wonder about them (not confusion, genuine curiosity)
4. **Coherent Complexity** - Contradictions make human sense (Kim Wexler archetype)

**Ensemble breadth matters.** 5-6 exceptional characters > 1 great lead.

## üé¨ VISUAL SPECTACLE vs CINEMATOGRAPHY

**CRITICAL DISTINCTION:**
- **Cinematography** = HOW it's shot (Breaking Bad desert = 9)
- **Visual Spectacle** = WHAT is shown (suburban houses, parking lots = 6)

**Visual Spectacle Scale:**
- 5 = Offices, homes, streets (everyday)
- 6-7 = Period settings, good production design
- 8 = Large practical sets, memorable VFX
- 9-10 = Unprecedented, iconic, pause-to-admire

3. **Recency bias is real.** Compare against anchors, not last show watched.

5. **Productive mystery > explained.** Kim Wexler's ambiguity > stated motives.

## üìù CATEGORY SCORING GUIDE

**Characters & Acting:**
- 9.5+ = Breaking Bad ensemble (Walter, Jesse, Gus, Mike, Hank all exceptional)
- 8-9 = Several memorable, some productive mystery
- 6-7 = Functional, well-acted but forgettable
- 5 = Plot vessels, no interiority

**World Building:**
- 10 = The Wire (Baltimore as living organism across institutions)
- 9 = Game of Thrones (civilizations with histories)
- 7 = Breaking Bad (real but not expansive)
- 5 = Generic backdrop, no texture

**Cinematography:**
- 9-10 = Single-frame recognizable, studied in film schools
- 7 = Competent, occasional inspiration
- 5 = Workmanlike, TV of its era

**Visual Spectacle:**
- 9-10 = Dragons that feel real, unprecedented scale
- 7 = Desert vistas, distinctive imagery
- 5 = Suburbs, offices, everyday settings

**Narrative Drive:**
- 10 = Breaking Bad (relentlessly compelling, near-perfect pacing)
- 7-8 = Slow burn that rewards patience
- 5 = Episodic, some filler

**Conceptual Density:**
- 10 = Multiple philosophical/political/social ideas woven coherently
- 7-8 = Several themes explored well
- 5 = Single theme or surface-level

**Narrative Path & Resolution:**
- 10 = Ozymandias-level payoff, earned, devastating
- 7-8 = Sticks landing, no cheap reversals
- 5 = Rushed, unresolved, or undermined
