## What It Feels Like to Watch

The Bear drops you onto the kitchen floor during the lunch rush and doesn't let you up for air. The sensory assault of sizzling pans, shouted orders, and clattering plates creates an almost unbearable tension that somehow becomes addictive. It's a show about trauma and ambition, family dysfunction and culinary artistry, where the difference between success and disaster is measured in seconds and the wrong sauce can ruin everything.
