Korean Fantasy Romance Perfected

Goblin is kdrama at its most ambitious—a fantasy romance about a 939-year-old goblin seeking to end his immortality by finding his human bride. Writer Kim Eun-sook created a phenomenon that transcends the genre, blending mythology, humor, tragedy, and genuine chemistry into sixteen hours of compulsive television.

The Goblin and His Bride

Gong Yoo plays Kim Shin, a Goryeo general cursed with immortality for sins he didn't commit. Kim Go-eun is Ji Eun-tak, the high school student who can see ghosts and might be the one to finally end his suffering. Their romance shouldn't work—he's nearly a millennium old, she's still in school—but the writing earns every moment through charm, pain, and genuine connection.

The Grim Reaper

Lee Dong-wook's Grim Reaper is the show's secret weapon. Amnesiac, awkward, inexplicably wearing suits, his storyline intersects with the goblin's in ways that reveal centuries of connected trauma. His romance with Sunny (Yoo In-na) provides both comic relief and genuine tragedy.

Visual Poetry

The production values rival feature films. Quebec City locations stand in for the goblin's Canadian wanderings. The historical flashbacks to Goryeo are rendered with period detail that grounds the fantasy in real suffering. Every frame could be a painting.

The Ending

Without spoiling specifics: the resolution walks a knife edge between satisfying and devastating. It earns its tears through investment, not manipulation. Not everyone gets a happy ending. Some get something more complicated.

Verdict: Peak kdrama. A fantasy romance that respects its audience and devastates them anyway.
