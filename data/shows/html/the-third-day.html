Folk Horror on Osea Island

The Third Day is immersive horror—six episodes split into "Summer" and "Winter," with a 12-hour live broadcast in between. Jude Law and Florence Pugh headline this tale of an island where old rituals persist and outsiders are not welcome. The Wicker Man by way of Midsommar.

Summer: Sam's Descent

Jude Law plays Sam, who rescues a girl on the causeway and finds himself trapped on Osea. The islanders are welcoming but strange, their customs ancient but meaningful. Law's performance captures a man slowly realizing he's in a trap—but the trap keeps changing shape.

Winter: Helen's Arrival

Florence Pugh's Helen arrives months later with her daughters, seeking the same island for different reasons. By then, we know something's wrong—but watching Helen discover it is its own journey. Pugh is as good here as in anything she's done.

The Live Episode

Between the two stories, HBO broadcast 12 continuous hours of live footage—Jude Law wandering the island, interacting with the cult, building toward the climax. It was an experiment that mostly worked, even if viewers didn't watch in real time.

The Ending Problem

The resolution score reflects an ending that leans more on shock than sense. The mythology never quite coheres, and the final images feel more designed than earned. But the journey is immersive enough that the destination almost doesn't matter.

Verdict: Atmosphere over coherence. A folk horror experiment that mostly succeeds.
